# Subsequent Events Review

Review and evaluate events occurring between the balance sheet date and the date of the auditor's report per SA 560 (Subsequent Events), classify them as adjusting or non-adjusting, and determine required financial statement treatment.

## Description

SA 560 (Subsequent Events) requires the auditor to perform procedures to identify events between the balance sheet date and the audit report date that may require adjustment or disclosure in the financial statements. This is NOT a one-time review at the end — it requires ongoing vigilance throughout the audit period and clear understanding of THREE DISTINCT PERIODS with different auditor obligations.

**Key principle:** Event classification (adjusting vs. non-adjusting) is NOT always binary. It requires professional judgment based on the critical question: "Did the condition EXIST at balance sheet date (adjusting), or did it ARISE after balance sheet date (non-adjusting)?" Borderline cases require investigation of timing and gradual vs. sudden deterioration.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Review Period Determination (Three-Period Framework per SA 560)

**SA 560 establishes THREE DISTINCT PERIODS with different auditor obligations:**

**Period 1: Balance Sheet Date to Audit Report Date** (SA 560.6-9)
- **Timeframe:** [Balance sheet date] to [Expected audit report date] = [X] days/months
- **Auditor obligation:** ACTIVE PROCEDURES REQUIRED — Auditor must perform procedures to identify events requiring adjustment or disclosure
- **Nature of procedures:** Inquiries of management, reading board minutes, reviewing interim financials, checking legal correspondence
- **Documentation:** All procedures performed during Period 1 must be documented with dates and findings

**Period 2: Audit Report Date to Financial Statement Issuance Date** (SA 560.10-13)
- **Timeframe:** [Audit report date] to [Financial statement issuance date (AGM approval/board approval)]
- **Auditor obligation:** NO OBLIGATION to perform procedures, BUT if facts become known that would have caused modification of audit report, auditor must take action
- **Action required:** Discuss with management, determine if financial statements need amendment, consider effect on audit report
- **Practical note:** Auditor should request management representation that any subsequent events in this period will be communicated

**Period 3: After Financial Statement Issuance** (SA 560.14-17)
- **Timeframe:** After [Financial statement issuance date]
- **Auditor obligation:** NO OBLIGATION to perform procedures, BUT if facts become known that existed at audit report date and would have caused modification, auditor must take action
- **Action required:** Discuss with management and TCWG, determine if financial statements need revision, assess effect on issued audit report (may require dual dating or new report)

**Document review period clearly:**

### Step 3: Event Classification with Judgment Guidance

For EACH event identified in Step 2, classify as Adjusting (Type I) or Non-Adjusting (Type II) per Ind AS 10.

**CRITICAL QUESTION: Did the condition EXIST at balance sheet date, or ARISE after?**

---

**A. ADJUSTING EVENTS (Type I) — Conditions EXISTED at Balance Sheet Date**

**Definition:** Events that provide evidence of conditions that EXISTED at the balance sheet date. Financial statements must be ADJUSTED to reflect these events.

**Examples with Judgment Discussion:**

1. **Customer bankruptcy shortly after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Customer ABC filed for bankruptcy on [date, X days after year-end], balance outstanding Rs. Y cr
   - **Judgment required:** When did deterioration begin?
     - If customer was already defaulting at year-end (invoices >90 days overdue) → Likely ADJUSTING (condition existed)
     - If customer paid on time through year-end but sudden bankruptcy occurred → Investigate (gradual vs. sudden)
   - **Evidence to examine:** Customer payment history, aging at year-end, industry conditions at year-end, bankruptcy filing details (sudden crisis vs. prolonged insolvency)
   - **Classification:** [Adjusting / Non-adjusting] because [rationale]
   - **Action:** [Adjust receivable and ECL provision / Disclose as non-adjusting event]

2. **Settlement of lawsuit after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Lawsuit settled on [date] for Rs. X cr (provision in financial statements: Rs. Y cr)
   - **Judgment required:** Was the obligation established at year-end?
     - Settlement amount provides evidence about the liability that existed at year-end → ADJUSTING
     - But if settlement is significantly different from year-end assessment, investigate why (new evidence emerged? Change in legal strategy?)
   - **Classification:** ADJUSTING (settlement provides evidence about year-end obligation)
   - **Action:** Adjust provision from Rs. Y to Rs. X

3. **Inventory NRV confirmed by post-year-end sales:**
   - **Event:** Inventory items with carrying value Rs. X sold post-year-end for Rs. Y (below cost)
   - **Judgment:** Sale below cost confirms NRV impairment existed at year-end → ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Write down inventory to NRV if not already done

4. **Fraud or error discovered after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Fraud involving [nature] discovered in [month], affecting year-end financial statements by Rs. X
   - **Judgment:** Fraud existed at year-end even if discovered later → ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Adjust financial statements, consider fraud reporting requirements

5. **Tax assessment received after year-end for prior year:**
   - **Event:** Tax assessment for FY[X] received in [month] with demand of Rs. Y cr
   - **Judgment:** Assessment relates to transactions in prior year, obligation existed at year-end → ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Assess provision adequacy, adjust if shortfall

---

**B. NON-ADJUSTING EVENTS (Type II) — Conditions AROSE After Balance Sheet Date**

**Definition:** Events indicative of conditions that AROSE after the balance sheet date. Financial statements are NOT adjusted, but DISCLOSURE REQUIRED if material.

**Examples:**

1. **Fire or natural disaster destroying plant after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Factory destroyed by fire on [date], asset carrying value Rs. X cr, insurance coverage Rs. Y cr
   - **Judgment:** Fire occurred after year-end, condition did not exist at balance sheet date → NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Disclosure required (see Step 4 for draft disclosure)

2. **Decline in market value of investments after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Investment in [entity] declined from Rs. X (year-end fair value) to Rs. Y (current market value)
   - **Judgment:** Market decline after balance sheet date → NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Disclosure required if material

3. **Major acquisition or disposal after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Acquired [entity] for Rs. X cr on [date] / Disposed of division for Rs. Y cr
   - **Judgment:** Transaction entered after year-end → NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Disclosure required (see Step 4)

4. **Issue of shares or debentures after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Rights issue of Rs. X cr completed on [date]
   - **Judgment:** Capital raised after year-end → NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Classification:** NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Disclosure required

5. **Litigation filed after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Lawsuit filed on [date] claiming Rs. X cr
   - **Judgment:** Cause of action arose after year-end → NON-ADJUSTING (but if related to pre-year-end transaction, investigate)
   - **Classification:** NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Disclosure as contingent liability

6. **Restructuring announced after year-end:**
   - **Event:** Board approved restructuring plan on [date] involving retrenchment of [X] employees, estimated cost Rs. Y cr
   - **Judgment:** Restructuring decision made after year-end → NON-ADJUSTING (no constructive obligation at year-end per Ind AS 37)
   - **Classification:** NON-ADJUSTING
   - **Action:** Disclosure required

---

**C. JUDGMENT CASES (NOT BINARY — Requires Investigation)**

**These cases are not clearly adjusting or non-adjusting and require professional judgment based on investigation:**

**Case 1: Customer bankruptcy soon after year-end with mixed indicators**

| Timing | Evidence Available | Analysis |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| **2 days after year-end** | Customer was >120 days overdue at year-end; Industry showing stress; Customer's financials showed negative net worth | **Likely ADJUSTING** — deterioration was gradual and evident at year-end, bankruptcy was culmination of existing condition |
| **1 week after year-end** | Customer paid invoice dated December 28 on January 3; Bankruptcy followed sudden factory fire on January 8 | **Investigate further** — payment history suggests solvency at year-end, but need to assess whether fire was insured and whether customer was already stressed |
| **8 months after year-end** | Customer paid on time through year-end and Q1; Industry downturn in Q2 led to bankruptcy | **Likely NON-ADJUSTING** — condition arose post-year-end, customer was solvent at balance sheet date |

**Case 2: Payment received post-year-end, then customer bankrupted months later**

- **Scenario:** Customer ABC, balance Rs. 50 lakhs at year-end, paid Rs. 30 lakhs in February, filed bankruptcy in July
- **Judgment:** Payment in February suggests receivable was good at year-end → Treat February payment as evidence supporting recoverability. Bankruptcy in July is a new condition → Classify July bankruptcy as NON-ADJUSTING
- **Action:** No adjustment to year-end receivable (payment supports recoverability); July bankruptcy is subsequent non-adjusting event (disclosure may be required if entity still has Rs. 20 lakhs exposure)

**Case 3: Gradual vs. Sudden Deterioration**

| Factor | Gradual Deterioration (Adjusting) | Sudden Shock (Non-Adjusting) |
|--------|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Payment pattern** | Progressively slower (30 days → 60 days → 90 days → default) | Paid on time until sudden default |
| **Industry conditions** | Sector stress evident at year-end | Sector healthy, sudden regulatory change post-year-end |
| **Financial health indicators** | Negative trends visible at year-end (declining margins, increasing leverage) | Sudden event (fire, cyberattack, key customer loss) |
| **Classification guidance** | Likely ADJUSTING — conditions existed and were worsening | Likely NON-ADJUSTING — sudden external shock |

**Decision process for borderline cases:**

1. **Investigate timing of deterioration:** When did the underlying condition first arise?
2. **Examine evidence available at year-end:** Was there information available at year-end suggesting the outcome?
3. **Consider gradual vs. sudden nature:** Gradual decline suggests condition existed; sudden shock suggests new condition
4. **Apply professional skepticism:** Is management arguing non-adjusting to avoid recording loss? Challenge their assertions
5. **Document judgment:** Explain WHY the event is classified as adjusting or non-adjusting with specific evidence

### Step 5: Impact on Audit Report

Assess whether subsequent events affect the audit report.

**Scenario 1: Adjusting events properly reflected in financial statements**
- Financial statements have been adjusted for all adjusting events identified
- **Audit report impact:** None — standard unmodified opinion

**Scenario 2: Non-adjusting events adequately disclosed**
- All material non-adjusting events are disclosed per Ind AS 10 with nature and financial effect
- **Audit report impact:** None — standard unmodified opinion

**Scenario 3: Non-adjusting events NOT adequately disclosed**
- Material non-adjusting events identified but not disclosed or disclosure inadequate
- **Audit report impact:** Qualified opinion per SA 705 (inadequate disclosure)
- **Basis for Qualified Opinion:** "The Company has not disclosed [event description] that occurred on [date]. Ind AS 10 requires disclosure of material non-adjusting events with nature and financial effect. In our opinion, except for the inadequate disclosure described above, the financial statements give a true and fair view..."

**Scenario 4: Adjusting events NOT reflected in financial statements (management refuses to adjust)**
- Adjusting events identified but management disagrees and refuses to adjust
- **Audit report impact:** Qualified or Adverse opinion per SA 705 (misstatement) depending on materiality
- **Example:** Customer bankruptcy on [date] provides evidence receivable of Rs. X cr was impaired at year-end, but management has not written off → Uncorrected misstatement → Qualify if material

**Scenario 5: Events affecting going concern**
- Events occurring after year-end that affect going concern assessment (loss of major customer, financing not obtained, debt default)
- **Cross-reference to /audit:going-concern:** Reassess going concern conclusion
- **Audit report impact:** If material uncertainty exists due to subsequent events, separate "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern" section required per SA 570

**Scenario 6: Events discovered after audit report date but before financial statement issuance (Period 2)**
- Facts became known during Period 2 that would have caused modification of audit report
- **Auditor action:** Discuss with management, request amendment of financial statements, withdraw previous audit report if necessary, issue new audit report (dual date or fully revised date)

**Scenario 7: Events discovered after financial statement issuance (Period 3)**
- Facts became known after issuance that existed at audit report date and would have caused modification
- **Auditor action:** Discuss with management and TCWG, assess whether financial statements need revision, consider effect on issued report (may require notification to users, dual dating, or new report)

**Audit Report Determination for This Entity:**

- [ ] **Scenario 1** — Adjusting events properly reflected, non-adjusting events adequately disclosed → Unmodified opinion, no modification
- [ ] **Scenario 3** — Non-adjusting event [describe] not disclosed → Qualified opinion for inadequate disclosure
- [ ] **Scenario 4** — Adjusting event [describe] not reflected → Qualified/Adverse opinion for misstatement
- [ ] **Scenario 5** — Events affecting going concern → Material Uncertainty section required (cross-reference /audit:going-concern)

## Reference to Standards

**SA 560 - Subsequent Events:**
- Para 6-9: Auditor's procedures to identify subsequent events (Period 1: Balance sheet date to audit report date)
- Para 10-13: Facts becoming known after audit report date but before financial statement issuance (Period 2)
- Para 14-17: Facts becoming known after financial statement issuance (Period 3)

**Ind AS 10 - Events After the Reporting Period:**
- Para 3-7: Adjusting events - provide evidence about conditions existing at balance sheet date
- Para 8-11: Non-adjusting events - indicative of conditions arising after balance sheet date, disclosure required if material
- Para 21: Disclosure requirements for non-adjusting events (nature and estimate of financial effect)
