# Fraud Risk

Assess fraud risk per SA 240 (The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud in an Audit of Financial Statements), identifying fraud risk factors using the fraud triangle framework, evaluating presumed fraud risks, and designing responsive audit procedures. Incorporates ISA 240 (Revised) enhancements effective December 2026.

## Required Context

Before running this command, ensure:
- Understanding of entity's business and industry
- Knowledge of management and governance structure
- Prior year audit findings (if continuing engagement)
- Understanding of internal controls and control environment
- Financial statement analytical review completed
- Engagement team members list (for team discussion documentation)
- Industry-specific fraud indicators (varies by sector)
- Whistleblower program/ethics hotline information (ISA 240 Revised requirement)

**Ask the user to provide:**
1. Names and roles of engagement team members for fraud discussion (SA 240 requires discussion with all key team members including engagement partner)
2. Whether entity has a whistleblower program or ethics hotline; if yes, any reports received during the period
3. Any known or suspected fraud incidents (current or prior years)
4. Industry-specific fraud patterns for this sector (e.g., channel stuffing in FMCG, warranty manipulation in automotive, inventory fraud in retail)

## Workflow

### Step 1 - Conduct and Document Engagement Team Discussion on Fraud Risks

**SA 240 requirement:** The engagement team must hold a discussion on the susceptibility of the entity's financial statements to material misstatement due to fraud. This discussion must involve the engagement partner and all key team members.

**Engagement Team Discussion Documentation Template:**

| Element | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Date of discussion** | [Date - typically during planning phase] |
| **Participants** | [Names and roles - MUST include engagement partner and all key team members: partner, manager, senior auditors] |
| **How and where financial statements may be susceptible to fraud** | [Specific areas discussed based on entity understanding - be specific, not generic] |
| **Revenue recognition fraud - how it might occur at THIS entity** | [Entity-specific scenarios: e.g., "Risk of recording sales before goods dispatched due to pressure to meet quarterly targets"; not generic "revenue may be overstated"] |
| **Management override - how it might occur at THIS entity** | [Entity-specific scenarios: e.g., "Manual journal entries to reclassify expenses as capital to improve profitability ratios for loan covenant compliance"; not generic "management may override controls"] |
| **Areas of significant estimates with fraud potential** | [Specific estimates: e.g., "Expected credit loss provision - management may use optimistic assumptions to avoid recognizing losses"] |
| **Unusual transactions to be alert for** | [Types: e.g., "Related party transactions near year-end", "Large credit notes issued post year-end", "Non-standard contract terms"] |
| **Known fraud indicators observed** | [Observations from understanding entity: e.g., "High turnover in finance team", "Loan covenant compliance is marginal", "Management bonus tied to profit targets"] |
| **Professional skepticism emphasis** | [Document reminder to team: "Maintain heightened skepticism throughout audit, particularly for areas with high fraud risk; question management representations and seek corroborating evidence"] |

**Ask the user:** "Document the engagement team fraud discussion. Provide specific details for each element above. The discussion must be substantive and entity-specific - generic statements will not satisfy SA 240 requirements. What areas were identified as susceptible to material misstatement due to fraud at THIS entity?"

**SA 240 emphasis:** The discussion should involve "setting aside beliefs that management and those charged with governance are honest and have integrity" and considering how fraud might be perpetrated and concealed.

### Step 2 - Understand Entity's Whistleblower Program (ISA 240 Revised Enhancement)

**ISA 240 (Revised) new requirement (effective December 15, 2026):** Auditor must obtain understanding of entity's whistleblower/ethics reporting mechanisms.

**Whistleblower Program Assessment:**

1. **Does the entity have a whistleblower program or ethics hotline?**
   - If YES: Who operates it (internal/third-party)? Who receives reports (audit committee/management/HR)?
   - If NO: Document absence and consider implications for control environment

2. **How are whistleblower reports handled?**
   - Investigation process? Escalation to audit committee? Protection for whistleblowers?

3. **Were any reports received during the period?**
   - If YES: Nature of reports? Were they investigated? Outcomes?
   - If NO: Is this absence credible given entity size and operations?

4. **Implications for fraud risk assessment:**
   - Absence of whistleblower program = weaker control environment (attitude/rationalization indicator)
   - Reports received but not investigated = red flag (management attitude concern)
   - Functional program with appropriate follow-up = positive control environment signal

**Ask the user:** "Does the entity have a whistleblower program? If yes, provide details on how it operates and whether any reports were received. If no, document the absence and consider impact on overall control environment assessment."

### Step 3 - Evaluate Fraud Risk Factors Under the Fraud Triangle

**SA 240 framework:** Fraud risk factors are evaluated under three categories representing conditions generally present when fraud occurs.

#### A. Incentive/Pressure Indicators

Circumstances that provide incentive or pressure to commit fraud:

**Financial targets and external pressures:**
- Loan covenants requiring minimum financial ratios (DSCR, debt-equity, interest coverage)
- Management KPIs and performance targets (budgets, analyst expectations, board-set targets)
- External pressures (IPO preparation, fundraising rounds, regulatory compliance deadlines, tax assessments)
- Personal financial pressure on management (compensation tied to performance, stock options approaching vesting, personal guarantees on entity debt)

**Financial stability threats:**
- Recurring losses or declining profitability
- Going concern issues or liquidity constraints
- Significant debt maturities approaching
- Adverse industry or economic conditions

**Ask the user:** "What financial targets exist for this entity? (Provide: loan covenant terms, management KPI metrics, analyst expectations, upcoming funding needs) Is management compensation tied to entity performance? Are there any personal financial pressures on key management personnel?"

**Overall Incentive/Pressure Level:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Rationale with entity-specific evidence:** [Document specific pressures identified]

#### B. Opportunity Indicators

Circumstances that provide opportunity to commit fraud:

**Governance gaps:**
- Lack of independent directors or ineffective board oversight
- Audit committee absent, does not meet regularly, or lacks financial expertise
- Internal audit function absent or reports to CFO instead of audit committee
- Weak monitoring by those charged with governance

**IT and access controls:**
- Who can create/modify journal entries in the ERP system? Are there approval workflows?
- Can a single person initiate, record, and approve transactions?
- Are there system access logs and periodic reviews of user access?
- Can data be modified without audit trails?

**Complex structures and transactions:**
- Related party network (multiple related entities, unclear business purpose)
- Off-balance-sheet entities or special purpose vehicles
- Multi-layered corporate structures or operations in multiple jurisdictions
- Complex transactions (derivatives, structured finance, revenue arrangements with multiple elements)

**Weak internal controls:**
- Poor segregation of duties (common in small entities)
- Limited monitoring of controls
- Management override capability (system back-doors, journal entry privileges)

**Ask the user:** "Assess governance structure: Are there independent directors? Does the audit committee meet regularly and have financial expertise? Does internal audit exist and report to audit committee? Provide IT access control information: Who can create journal entries? Are there approval workflows in the ERP system? Are there any complex structures (related party network, off-balance-sheet entities)?"

**Overall Opportunity Level:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Rationale with entity-specific evidence:** [Document specific opportunities identified]

#### C. Attitude/Rationalization Indicators

Circumstances indicating attitude or rationalization allowing individuals to commit fraud:

**Management integrity concerns:**
- Previous regulatory issues (SEBI actions, RBI penalties, tax disputes)
- Litigation history (shareholder suits, customer disputes, employee claims)
- History of aggressive accounting choices or pushing boundaries on revenue recognition
- Known violations of laws or regulations

**Communication of ethics:**
- Does a code of conduct exist and is it enforced?
- Is ethics training provided to employees?
- What is the "tone at the top" - does management emphasize integrity and ethical behavior?
- Are there consequences for unethical behavior?

**Auditor relationship quality:**
- Is management cooperative or restrictive with audit requests?
- Are there unreasonable deadlines or time pressures?
- Have there been disputes on accounting treatments or proposed adjustments in prior years?
- Has management changed auditors frequently (auditor shopping)?

**Ask the user:** "Assess management integrity: Any history of regulatory issues, litigation, or aggressive accounting? Is there a code of conduct and ethics training program? How would you characterize the tone at the top? Is management cooperative with the audit or are there restrictions/disputes?"

**Overall Attitude/Rationalization Level:** [High / Medium / Low]
**Rationale with entity-specific evidence:** [Document specific attitudes/rationalizations identified]

### Step 4 - Assess Fraud Risk at Assertion Level (ISA 240 Revised Enhancement)

**ISA 240 (Revised) enhancement:** For areas identified as having fraud risk, assess at the assertion level (not just financial statement area level).

**Assertion-Level Fraud Risk Assessment:**

| Financial Statement Area | Assertion Most Susceptible to Fraud | Specific Fraud Risk Factor | Fraud Risk Assessment (H/M/L) |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Revenue | Occurrence | Fictitious sales recorded to meet targets; side agreements not disclosed; bill-and-hold arrangements | [H/M/L] |
| Revenue | Cut-off | Sales recorded in wrong period to shift revenue between quarters/years | [H/M/L] |
| Revenue | Valuation | Discounts/rebates not recorded; returns understated; variable consideration overstated | [H/M/L] |
| Receivables | Valuation | Expected credit loss (ECL) provision understated using optimistic assumptions | [H/M/L] |
| Inventory | Existence | Fictitious inventory to inflate assets; overstatement of quantities at year-end counts | [H/M/L] |
| Inventory | Valuation | Net realizable value (NRV) overstated; obsolescence not recognized | [H/M/L] |
| Journal Entries | Classification | Period-end manual entries to unusual account combinations to manipulate results | [H/M/L] |
| Journal Entries | Valuation | Manual entries to estimates (provisions, accruals) to manage earnings | [H/M/L] |
| Related Party Transactions | Valuation | Transactions not at arm's length to shift profits or assets | [H/M/L] |
| Related Party Transactions | Disclosure | Related party relationships or transactions not disclosed | [H/M/L] |

**Ask the user:** "For the significant financial statement areas at this entity, assess fraud risk at the assertion level using the table above. Which specific assertions are most susceptible to fraud and why?"

### Step 5 - Identify and Document Presumed Fraud Risks (SA 240 Requirements)

**SA 240 presumed risks:** The auditor shall presume that there are risks of fraud in revenue recognition AND that management override of controls risk is present in ALL audits.

#### Presumed Risk 1: Revenue Recognition Fraud Risk

**Default status:** INCLUDED in all audits (SA 240 presumption)

**Can this risk be rebutted?** YES, but ONLY with documented justification and engagement partner approval.

**Rebuttal criteria (all must be met):**
1. Revenue is simple and easily verifiable (e.g., single product line, cash sales, government contracts with fixed terms, regulated tariffs)
2. Fraud risk factors do NOT suggest revenue manipulation (no incentive/pressure related to revenue targets)
3. No history of revenue recognition issues in prior periods

**If rebutting revenue recognition fraud risk:**
- Document: "Revenue recognition fraud risk is rebutted because: [entity-specific rationale referencing simplicity of revenue streams and absence of fraud risk factors]"
- Document: "This rebuttal was reviewed and approved by [engagement partner name] on [date]"

**Decision point:** If you are considering rebutting the revenue recognition fraud risk, you MUST obtain engagement partner approval before proceeding. Document the partner's approval explicitly.

**If NOT rebutting (standard case):**

**Specific fraud risk for THIS entity:** [Describe how revenue recognition fraud might occur at this specific entity - be specific, not generic]

**Responsive procedures for revenue recognition fraud:**
1. Test revenue transactions around year-end for proper cut-off (guideline: 10 days before/after year-end, test 100% of large transactions >Rs. [threshold])
2. Examine unusual or non-standard sales terms (right of return, consignment, bill-and-hold arrangements, extended payment terms)
3. Analyze revenue by month/quarter for unusual patterns (spikes near period-end, reversals post period-end)
4. Confirm terms of significant transactions with customers (top 15-20 customers covering 60-70% of revenue)
5. Review manual journal entries affecting revenue accounts (especially period-end entries, entries by senior management, round-number entries)
6. For complex revenue arrangements: Review contracts for performance obligations, variable consideration, allocation of transaction price per Ind AS 115

#### Presumed Risk 2: Management Override of Controls

**Default status:** INCLUDED in ALL audits (SA 240 requirement - CANNOT be rebutted)

**Can this risk be rebutted?** NO. Management override risk exists in EVERY audit, regardless of control environment quality.

**SA 240 principle:** Even in entities with strong controls and ethical management, management CAN override those controls. This risk is ALWAYS present.

**Specific risk for THIS entity:** [Describe how management override might occur at this specific entity - consider what controls management has ability to override, what journal entry privileges exist, what estimates are subject to management bias]

**Required responsive procedures for management override (SA 240 mandatory procedures):**

1. **Test journal entries and other adjustments:**
   - Test appropriateness of journal entries recorded in the general ledger (focus on: manual entries, period-end entries, entries to unusual account combinations, entries made by senior management, round-number entries)
   - Guideline: Test ALL manual journal entries exceeding Rs. [threshold based on performance materiality], plus sample of smaller entries
   - Review journal entries for unusual accounts or account combinations
   - Test period-end adjustments (last 5-10 days of year)

2. **Review accounting estimates for management bias:**
   - Identify significant estimates (ECL, provisions, useful lives, fair values, actuarial assumptions)
   - Review for bias (are estimates consistently optimistic? do they achieve desired results like meeting covenants or targets?)
   - Perform retrospective review (compare prior year estimates to actual outcomes - pattern of optimism?)
   - Consider engaging specialist for complex estimates (valuation for fair values, actuary for employee benefits)

3. **Evaluate business rationale for significant unusual transactions:**
   - Identify transactions outside normal course of business (restructuring, asset disposals, related party transactions, derivatives)
   - Understand business rationale and economic substance
   - Assess whether accounting treatment is appropriate or aggressive
   - Consider whether transactions are designed to achieve particular accounting outcome

**THESE PROCEDURES CANNOT BE REDUCED OR OMITTED** (SA 240 mandatory requirement for management override risk in ALL audits)

### Step 6 - Overall Fraud Risk Assessment and Impact on Audit Approach

**Overall fraud risk level for this engagement:** [Low / Medium / High]

**Rationale:** [Based on fraud triangle assessment (incentive/pressure, opportunity, attitude/rationalization) and specific fraud risks identified]

**Impact on audit approach:**

**If Overall Fraud Risk = LOW:**
- Standard audit procedures per risk assessment
- No additional fraud-responsive procedures beyond SA 240 minimums (revenue recognition, management override)
- Standard supervision and review
- Maintain professional skepticism throughout

**If Overall Fraud Risk = MEDIUM:**
- Enhanced procedures for identified risk areas beyond SA 240 minimums
- Incorporate additional elements of unpredictability in procedure selection (vary timing, locations, procedures from prior year)
- Increased partner involvement (partner attends inventory observation, reviews key working papers, discusses significant matters with management)
- Enhanced team briefings emphasizing professional skepticism
- Consider more extensive journal entry testing or analytical procedures

**If Overall Fraud Risk = HIGH:**
- Extensive fraud-focused procedures for all high-risk areas
- Consider engaging forensic accounting specialist or fraud examiner
- Significant unpredictability elements (surprise visits, unannounced procedures, testing unusual periods)
- Partner deeply involved in planning and execution (not just review)
- Enhanced professional skepticism emphasized in team briefings and throughout audit
- Consider extended procedures (forensic data analytics, interviews with personnel beyond management, observation of operations)

**Decision point:** If overall fraud risk is assessed as HIGH, consider whether the engagement should be accepted/continued per SA 220 quality management requirements. High fraud risk may indicate unacceptable engagement risk. Document consultation with managing partner or ethics partner if continuing a high-fraud-risk engagement.

### Step 7 - Communication Requirements

**SA 240 communication requirements:**

**A. Communication to Management:**
- Communicate identified weaknesses in internal controls relevant to fraud prevention and detection
- If fraud is identified or suspected, communicate to appropriate level of management (UNLESS management is involved in the fraud)
- Request management's assessment of fraud risk and controls to prevent/detect fraud

**B. Communication to Those Charged with Governance (Board/Audit Committee):**
- Communicate any fraud identified during the audit (even if not material)
- Communicate any suspected fraud identified during the audit
- Communicate significant deficiencies in internal controls related to fraud
- Communicate fraud risk assessment process and identified fraud risks

**C. Communication to Regulators (if applicable):**
- **Section 143(12) of Companies Act 2013:** If auditor has reason to believe that an offense involving fraud is being or has been committed against the company by officers or employees, report to Central Government within prescribed time
- Applicability: If fraud amount is Rs. 1 crore or more (immediately report); if fraud is below Rs. 1 crore (report to audit committee first, then to Central Government if not addressed)
- **Reporting timeline:** Within 2 days of knowledge/suspicion (for frauds >Rs. 1 crore) or within 60 days (after giving audit committee opportunity to respond for frauds <Rs. 1 crore)

**Communication plan for this engagement:**

---

**Disclaimer:** This tool provides a structured framework for fraud risk assessment per SA 240, but does not substitute professional audit judgment or guarantee fraud detection. An audit conducted in accordance with SAs provides reasonable assurance (not absolute assurance) of detecting material misstatements due to fraud. The auditor's responsibility is to design procedures responsive to identified fraud risks, maintain professional skepticism, and exercise professional judgment throughout the audit. Fraud involving collusion or sophisticated concealment may not be detected even with proper audit procedures.
