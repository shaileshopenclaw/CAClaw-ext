# Indian Standards on Auditing (SAs) Reference

## Framework Overview

The **Auditing and Assurance Standards Board (AASB)** of the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) issues Standards on Auditing (SAs) that are mandatory for all statutory audits in India.

**Key Characteristics:**
- SA numbering follows International Standards on Auditing (ISA) numbering with Indian adaptations
- Each SA contains: Objective, Definitions, Requirements (mandatory - "shall"), and Application and Other Explanatory Material (guidance - "may")
- Requirements are mandatory; application material is explanatory
- Non-compliance requires documentation and justification

**SA Categories:**
- **200-299**: General Principles and Responsibilities
- **300-499**: Risk Assessment and Response to Assessed Risks
- **500-599**: Audit Evidence
- **600-699**: Using the Work of Others
- **700-799**: Audit Conclusions and Reporting
- **800-899**: Specialized Areas

## SA 200-299: General Principles and Responsibilities

### SA 200: Overall Objectives of the Independent Auditor

**Objective:** Conduct audit to obtain reasonable assurance that financial statements are free from material misstatement.

**Key Requirements:**
- Exercise professional judgment and maintain professional skepticism throughout the audit
- Comply with relevant ethical requirements including independence
- Plan and perform audit to obtain reasonable assurance (not absolute assurance)
- Identify and assess risks of material misstatement through understanding entity and environment
- Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to reduce audit risk to acceptably low level
- Form opinion based on audit evidence and express clearly through written report

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - establishes fundamental principles

---

### SA 210: Agreeing the Terms of Audit Engagements

**Objective:** Accept or continue audit only when basis is agreed and documented.

**Key Requirements:**
- Establish whether preconditions for audit exist (acceptable financial reporting framework, management acknowledges responsibilities)
- Agree terms of engagement with management/those charged with governance
- Document agreed terms in audit engagement letter or other suitable form
- For recurring audits, evaluate whether circumstances require revised terms

**Management Responsibilities to Document:**
- Preparation of financial statements per applicable framework
- Design, implementation, and maintenance of internal controls
- Providing auditor with access to all information, additional information requested, and unrestricted access to persons

**When It Applies:** Every new audit engagement; re-evaluation for recurring engagements

---

### SA 220: Quality Control for an Audit of Financial Statements

**Objective:** Implement quality control procedures at engagement level.

**Key Requirements:**
- Engagement partner assumes responsibility for overall quality on each audit
- Maintain independence and evaluate threats throughout engagement
- Form conclusion on compliance with independence requirements and take appropriate action
- Assign engagement team with appropriate competence and capabilities
- Perform engagement in accordance with professional standards and regulatory requirements
- Ensure direction, supervision, and review of engagement team work
- Conduct engagement quality control review for certain engagements (e.g., listed entities)

**Documentation:**
- Issues identified with respect to compliance with ethical requirements
- Conclusions on independence
- Conclusions on acceptance and continuance
- Nature and scope of consultations and resulting conclusions

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement

---

### SA 230: Audit Documentation

**Objective:** Prepare documentation that provides sufficient and appropriate record of the basis for the auditor's report and evidence that audit was planned and performed per SAs.

**Key Requirements:**
- Prepare audit documentation on timely basis
- Document significant matters requiring professional judgment and conclusions
- Document discussions of significant matters with management, those charged with governance, and others
- Document how inconsistencies with final conclusions were addressed
- Assemble final audit file within 60 days of auditor's report date
- Do not delete or discard documentation before end of retention period (minimum 7 years from report date per Companies Act)

**Documentation Must Include:**
- Identifying characteristics of items or matters tested
- Who performed the work and date completed
- Who reviewed the work and date and extent of review
- Reasons for departures from relevant requirements and alternative procedures performed

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - ongoing documentation requirement

---

### SA 240: The Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud

**Objective:** Identify and assess risks of material misstatement due to fraud, obtain sufficient appropriate evidence, and respond appropriately.

**Key Requirements:**
- Maintain professional skepticism recognizing possibility of material misstatement due to fraud
- Discuss among engagement team susceptibility of entity's financial statements to material misstatement due to fraud
- Make inquiries of management and others regarding fraud risks and internal controls
- Identify and assess risks of material misstatement due to fraud at financial statement and assertion levels
- **Presumption of fraud risk in revenue recognition** (rebuttable)
- Treat assessed risks of material misstatement due to fraud as significant risks
- Design and implement responses to address assessed fraud risks
- Evaluate whether unusual or unexpected relationships identified indicate fraud risk
- If fraud identified or suspected, communicate to appropriate level of management and those charged with governance

**Two Types of Fraud:**
- **Fraudulent financial reporting** - intentional misstatements to deceive users
- **Misappropriation of assets** - theft of entity's assets

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement

---

### SA 250: Consideration of Laws and Regulations in an Audit

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with laws and regulations having direct effect on financial statements, and perform specified procedures regarding other laws and regulations.

**Key Requirements:**
- Obtain general understanding of legal and regulatory framework and how entity complies
- Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding compliance with laws having direct effect on determination of material amounts and disclosures (e.g., tax laws, Companies Act accounting provisions)
- Perform specified audit procedures to identify non-compliance with other laws that may have material effect on financial statements
- Request written representations that management has disclosed all known actual or possible non-compliance
- If non-compliance identified, obtain understanding of nature and circumstances, and evaluate effect on financial statements
- Communicate non-compliance to those charged with governance unless clearly inconsequential

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement

---

### SA 260: Communication with Those Charged with Governance

**Objective:** Communicate with those charged with governance regarding audit responsibilities, planned scope and timing, and significant findings.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine appropriate persons with whom to communicate
- Communicate auditor's responsibilities in relation to financial statement audit
- Communicate planned scope and timing of audit (without compromising audit effectiveness)
- Communicate significant findings from audit including:
  - Auditor's views on qualitative aspects of accounting practices
  - Significant difficulties encountered during audit
  - Significant matters discussed with management
  - Written representations requested
  - Other matters significant to oversight of financial reporting process
- Communicate significant deficiencies in internal control identified during audit

**Form and Timing:**
- In writing when oral communication inadequate
- Timely communication to enable appropriate action
- For listed entities, communicate in writing

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement

---

### SA 265: Communicating Deficiencies in Internal Control

**Objective:** Communicate appropriately to those charged with governance and management deficiencies in internal control identified during audit.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine whether deficiencies identified constitute significant deficiencies
- Communicate in writing significant deficiencies in internal control to those charged with governance on timely basis
- Communicate to management at appropriate level of responsibility deficiencies not communicated to management by other parties (on timely basis, in writing if significant)
- Include in written communication:
  - Description of deficiencies and explanation of potential effects
  - Sufficient information to enable governance and management to understand context

**Significant Deficiency Defined:** Deficiency or combination of deficiencies in internal control that is of sufficient importance to merit attention of those charged with governance.

**When It Applies:** When deficiencies identified during audit (any audit engagement)

## SA 300-499: Risk Assessment and Response to Assessed Risks

### SA 300: Planning an Audit of Financial Statements

**Objective:** Plan the audit to be performed in an effective manner.

**Key Requirements:**
- Develop overall audit strategy setting scope, timing, and direction
- Develop audit plan including nature, timing, and extent of audit procedures
- Plan audit work so more attention is devoted to areas of higher risk
- Update and change strategy and plan as necessary during audit
- Plan nature, timing, and extent of direction and supervision of engagement team and review of their work
- Consider results of preliminary engagement activities and knowledge from other engagements
- Ascertain reporting objectives to plan timing and communication

**Matters to Consider in Strategy:**
- Entity characteristics, reporting framework, industry-specific requirements
- Assessment of materiality per SA 320
- Areas where higher risk of material misstatement
- Impact of IT environment
- Availability of client personnel and data

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - planning phase

---

### SA 315: Identifying and Assessing the Risks of Material Misstatement

**Objective:** Identify and assess risks of material misstatement (whether due to fraud or error) at financial statement and assertion levels through understanding entity and its environment including internal control.

**Key Requirements:**
- Perform risk assessment procedures: inquiries of management and others, analytical procedures, observation and inspection
- Understand entity and environment including:
  - Industry, regulatory, and external factors
  - Nature of entity including operations, ownership, governance, investments, financing
  - Entity's selection and application of accounting policies
  - Entity's objectives, strategies, and related business risks
  - Measurement and review of financial performance
- Obtain understanding of internal control components relevant to audit:
  - Control environment
  - Entity's risk assessment process
  - Information system and communication
  - Control activities
  - Monitoring of controls
- Identify and assess risks of material misstatement at financial statement level and assertion level for classes of transactions, account balances, and disclosures
- Determine which risks are significant risks requiring special audit consideration
- **Evaluate design and determine implementation of controls** relevant to risks for which substantive procedures alone cannot provide sufficient appropriate evidence

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - risk assessment phase

---

### SA 320: Materiality in Planning and Performing an Audit

**Objective:** Apply concept of materiality appropriately in planning and performing audit.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine materiality for financial statements as a whole when establishing overall audit strategy
- Determine performance materiality (set lower than materiality for financial statements as a whole to reduce probability that aggregate uncorrected and undetected misstatements exceed materiality)
- Determine materiality level or levels for particular classes of transactions, account balances, or disclosures if warranted (e.g., related party transactions, key management compensation)
- Revise materiality if becoming aware of information that would have caused different determination initially

**Common Benchmarks:**
- 0.5% to 1% of revenue
- 1% to 2% of total assets
- 5% to 10% of profit before tax
- 1% to 2% of equity

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - planning and execution phases

---

### SA 330: The Auditor's Responses to Assessed Risks

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding assessed risks of material misstatement through designing and implementing appropriate responses.

**Key Requirements:**
- Design and implement overall responses to address assessed risks at financial statement level
- Design and perform further audit procedures (tests of controls and substantive procedures) responsive to assessed risks at assertion level
- For significant risks: perform substantive procedures specifically responsive to that risk
- For risks requiring substantive procedures alone cannot provide sufficient evidence: test operating effectiveness of controls
- Perform substantive procedures for each material class of transactions, account balance, and disclosure
- Consider timing of procedures, extent of procedures, and whether to perform substantive analytical procedures or tests of details
- Evaluate whether external confirmation procedures are to be performed as substantive audit procedures

**Substantive Procedures Must Include:**
- Agreeing or reconciling financial statements with underlying accounting records
- Examining material journal entries and other adjustments made during preparation of financial statements

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - execution phase

---

### SA 402: Audit Considerations Relating to an Entity Using a Service Organization

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence when entity uses services of service organization.

**Key Requirements:**
- Obtain understanding of services provided by service organization and their effect on internal control relevant to audit
- Determine whether sufficient appropriate understanding of controls at service organization to assess risks
- Design and perform audit procedures responsive to assessed risks (may include obtaining Type I or Type II report from service organization auditor)
- If using service auditor's report, evaluate whether it provides sufficient appropriate evidence

**Service Organization Examples:** Payroll processing, IT hosting, share registry, fund management

**When It Applies:** When entity uses third-party service organization affecting financial statements

---

### SA 450: Evaluation of Misstatements Identified during the Audit

**Objective:** Evaluate effect of identified misstatements on audit and effect of uncorrected misstatements on financial statements.

**Key Requirements:**
- Accumulate misstatements identified during audit (other than clearly trivial)
- Determine whether overall audit strategy and audit plan need modification if nature and circumstances of misstatements indicate other misstatements may exist
- Communicate on timely basis all misstatements to appropriate level of management and request correction
- Prior to evaluation, request written representation from management whether they believe uncorrected misstatements are immaterial
- Determine whether uncorrected misstatements are material individually or in aggregate
- Communicate uncorrected misstatements and their effect to those charged with governance

**Clearly Trivial:** Matters clearly inconsequential (not "not material") - usually set at 3-5% of performance materiality

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - evaluation phase

---

## SA 500-599: Audit Evidence

### SA 500: Audit Evidence

**Objective:** Design and perform audit procedures to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to draw reasonable conclusions.

**Key Requirements:**
- Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to reduce audit risk to acceptably low level
- Consider relevance and reliability when evaluating audit evidence
- Use assertions in assessing risks and designing procedures (existence, completeness, accuracy, valuation, rights and obligations, presentation and disclosure)
- Design and perform audit procedures appropriate in circumstances for obtaining evidence
- If information contradicts or raises doubt about reliability, investigate and determine modifications needed to procedures
- If expert used, evaluate whether expert's work constitutes appropriate evidence

**Reliability Hierarchy (from most to least reliable):**
1. Evidence obtained directly by auditor (e.g., observation)
2. Evidence obtained from external sources (e.g., bank confirmations)
3. Evidence generated internally with strong controls
4. Documentary evidence (vs. oral)
5. Original documents (vs. copies)

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - guides all evidence gathering

---

### SA 501: Audit Evidence—Specific Considerations for Selected Items

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence regarding inventory, litigation and claims, and segment information.

**Key Requirements:**

**Inventory:**
- If material, attend physical inventory counting unless impracticable
- Evaluate management's instructions and procedures for recording and controlling results
- Observe performance of count procedures
- Inspect inventory
- Perform test counts
- If not attending, perform alternative procedures

**Litigation and Claims:**
- Inquire of management and others about existence
- Review board minutes and correspondence with entity's lawyers
- Request management to send letter of inquiry to entity's legal counsel

**Segment Information:**
- Obtain understanding of methods used and evaluate appropriateness
- Test application of methods
- Perform analytical procedures or other procedures as appropriate

**When It Applies:** When inventory is material, litigation exists, or segment reporting required

---

### SA 505: External Confirmations

**Objective:** Design and perform external confirmation procedures to obtain relevant and reliable audit evidence.

**Key Requirements:**
- When using external confirmation procedures, maintain control over selection, communication with respondents, and evaluation
- Determine information to be confirmed
- Select appropriate confirming party
- Design confirmation requests (including proper authorization for management to release information)
- Send requests including follow-up where applicable
- **For accounts receivable, use external confirmations unless**: total balance immaterial, confirmations ineffective, or combined assessed inherent and control risk is low and other substantive procedures address assessed risk
- If management refuses to allow confirmation, inquire as to reasons, evaluate validity, and seek corroboration; consider implications for assessment of fraud risk and for auditor's report
- Evaluate whether results provide relevant and reliable evidence or indicate need for additional procedures

**Confirmation Types:**
- **Positive confirmation** - respond whether or not agree with information
- **Negative confirmation** - respond only if disagree (provides less persuasive evidence)

**When It Applies:** Commonly for bank balances, receivables, payables, legal matters, inventory held by third parties

---

### SA 510: Initial Audit Engagements—Opening Balances

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about opening balances in initial engagement.

**Key Requirements:**
- Read most recent financial statements and predecessor auditor's report for information relevant to opening balances including disclosures, accounting policies, consistency
- Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether opening balances contain misstatements materially affecting current period statements:
  - Determine whether prior period closing balances correctly brought forward
  - Determine whether opening balances reflect appropriate accounting policies
  - Perform specific audit procedures to obtain evidence regarding opening balances
- Request management to authorize predecessor auditor to respond to inquiries and review predecessor's working papers

**If Unable to Obtain Sufficient Evidence:** Modify opinion per SA 705

**When It Applies:** First-year audits or when auditor changed

---

### SA 520: Analytical Procedures

**Objective:** Obtain relevant and reliable audit evidence when using substantive analytical procedures, and design and perform analytical procedures near end of audit.

**Key Requirements:**
- **Risk assessment procedures (mandatory):** Perform analytical procedures to assist in understanding entity and identifying areas of increased risk
- **Substantive analytical procedures (optional):** Design and perform when more effective/efficient than tests of details, including:
  - Determining suitability given assertions
  - Evaluating reliability of data
  - Developing expectation of recorded amounts/ratios
  - Determining amount of difference acceptable without further investigation
  - If investigation identifies misstatement or fraud, reassess assessed risks
- **Overall review (mandatory):** Perform analytical procedures at/near end to assist in forming overall conclusion on consistency of financial statements with auditor's understanding

**When It Applies:** Every audit - mandatory for risk assessment and final review; optional as substantive procedure

---

### SA 530: Audit Sampling

**Objective:** Design and perform audit sampling to provide reasonable basis for conclusions about population tested.

**Key Requirements:**
- Consider purpose of procedure and characteristics of population when designing sample
- Determine sample size sufficient to reduce sampling risk to acceptably low level
- Select items for sample in way that each unit has chance of selection
- For tests of controls, consider tolerable rate of deviation, expected rate, desired level of assurance
- For substantive procedures, consider tolerable misstatement, expected misstatement, desired level of assurance
- Perform audit procedures appropriate to purpose on each item selected
- Investigate nature and cause of deviations or misstatements and evaluate their effect on purpose and on other areas
- Project misstatements found in sample to population
- Evaluate results of audit sampling and whether use of sampling has provided reasonable basis for conclusions

**Sampling Risk:** Risk that auditor's conclusion based on sample may differ from conclusion if entire population examined

**When It Applies:** When using statistical or non-statistical sampling approaches

---

### SA 540: Auditing Accounting Estimates and Related Disclosures

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether accounting estimates and related disclosures are reasonable in context of applicable financial reporting framework.

**Key Requirements:**
- Obtain understanding of how management makes estimates and data on which they are based
- Determine whether accounting estimates give rise to significant risks
- For accounting estimates giving rise to significant risks, evaluate:
  - How management assessed estimation uncertainty
  - Whether accounting estimates are recognized or disclosed per framework
  - Reasonableness of management's decisions
  - Intention to carry out specific courses of action and ability to do so
- Test how management made estimate and data used, or develop independent estimate for comparison, or review subsequent events
- Evaluate whether accounting estimates are either reasonable or misstated
- Obtain written representation from management regarding reasonableness of significant assumptions

**Common Estimates:** Allowances for doubtful accounts, inventory obsolescence, warranty obligations, fair values, asset impairment, litigation reserves, pension obligations

**When It Applies:** Every audit involving accounting estimates (virtually all audits)

---

### SA 550: Related Parties

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about related party relationships and transactions, and evaluate their proper accounting and disclosure.

**Key Requirements:**
- Perform risk assessment procedures to obtain information relevant to identifying related party relationships and transactions
- Maintain alertness during audit for related party information when inspecting records or documents
- If identifies previously unidentified or undisclosed related parties or significant related party transactions, communicate to engagement team
- If identifies arrangements suggesting existence of related party relationships/transactions not disclosed, determine whether underlying circumstances confirm existence
- For identified significant related party transactions outside normal course of business:
  - Inspect underlying contracts or agreements
  - Evaluate whether business rationale suggests fraudulent financial reporting
  - Obtain audit evidence that transaction appropriately authorized and approved
- Evaluate whether related party relationships and transactions identified have been appropriately accounted for and disclosed per framework
- Obtain written representation from management and those charged with governance regarding completeness of related party information

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - Companies Act requires related party disclosures

---

### SA 560: Subsequent Events

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether events between date of financial statements and date of auditor's report requiring adjustment or disclosure are appropriately reflected.

**Key Requirements:**
- Perform procedures designed to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence that all events up to auditor's report date requiring adjustment or disclosure have been identified:
  - Obtain understanding of procedures management established
  - Inquire of management and those charged with governance
  - Read minutes of meetings held after period end
  - Read entity's latest subsequent interim financial statements
- Respond appropriately to facts becoming known after report date but before financial statements issued
- If management does not amend statements when auditor believes should be amended, modify opinion per SA 705

**Types of Subsequent Events:**
- **Adjusting events** - provide evidence of conditions existing at balance sheet date
- **Non-adjusting events** - indicative of conditions arising after balance sheet date (require disclosure if material)

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement

---

### SA 570: Going Concern

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about appropriateness of management's use of going concern basis, conclude whether material uncertainty exists, and determine implications for auditor's report.

**Key Requirements:**
- Evaluate management's assessment of entity's ability to continue as going concern
- Consider same period as used by management (at least 12 months from balance sheet date, or reporting date if longer)
- Consider whether management's assessment includes all relevant information
- Perform additional procedures if events or conditions identified indicating material uncertainty may exist
- Request written representations regarding management's plans and feasibility
- If adequate disclosure of material uncertainty made in statements, conclude appropriately and include Emphasis of Matter paragraph in report
- If adequate disclosure not made, express qualified or adverse opinion per SA 705
- If management unwilling to make or extend assessment when requested, consider implications for report

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement

---

### SA 580: Written Representations

**Objective:** Obtain written representations from management and, where appropriate, those charged with governance that they believe they have fulfilled their responsibility for preparation of financial statements and for completeness of information provided.

**Key Requirements:**
- Request written representations from management with appropriate responsibilities for financial statements
- Date representations as of date of auditor's report but covering all periods referred to in report
- Request representations regarding:
  - Management's responsibilities for financial statements
  - All information provided and completeness of transactions
  - Significant assumptions used in making accounting estimates are reasonable
  - Related party disclosures complete
  - Matters specific to entity or industry
  - Uncorrected misstatements are immaterial
- If management refuses to provide requested representations, discuss with management, re-evaluate integrity, take appropriate actions (including considering effect on auditor's opinion), and disclaim opinion or withdraw

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - obtained at end of fieldwork

---

## SA 600-699: Using the Work of Others

### SA 600: Using the Work of Another Auditor

**Objective:** When using work of another auditor, determine how work will affect the audit and whether sufficient appropriate audit evidence obtained.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine whether to make reference to work of other auditor in principal auditor's report
- If not making reference:
  - Perform procedures to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence that other auditor's work is adequate
  - Communicate with other auditor matters affecting their work
  - Assess other auditor's independence and professional competence
  - Review other auditor's working papers
- If making reference:
  - Indicate clearly division of responsibility in auditor's report
  - State magnitude of portion of financial statements audited by other auditor

**When It Applies:** Group audits with multiple locations or when subsidiaries audited by different firms

---

### SA 610: Using the Work of Internal Auditors

**Objective:** Determine whether internal audit function work can be used and extent to which it can be used.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine whether internal audit function's organizational status and relevant policies and procedures adequately support objectivity, level of competence, and whether systematic and disciplined approach applied
- If planning to use work of internal auditors, discuss planned use and coordinate activities
- Read internal audit reports to obtain understanding of work performed
- Determine whether, in which areas, and to what extent work can be used
- Re-perform some of internal auditors' work to evaluate adequacy
- External auditor has sole responsibility for opinion expressed - responsibility not reduced by use of internal audit work

**Cannot Rely on Internal Audit Alone For:** Significant risks, significant judgments, related party transactions

**When It Applies:** When entity has internal audit function and external auditor plans to use their work

---

### SA 620: Using the Work of an Auditor's Expert

**Objective:** Determine whether to use auditor's expert's work and if so, whether work is adequate for audit purposes.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine whether expertise needed and nature of that expertise
- Evaluate whether expert has necessary competence, capabilities, and objectivity
- Obtain understanding of field of expertise
- Agree in writing on nature, scope, and objectives of expert's work; respective roles and responsibilities; nature, timing, extent of communication; and confidentiality requirements
- Evaluate adequacy of expert's work including:
  - Relevance and reasonableness of findings/conclusions
  - Relevance and reasonableness of assumptions and methods
  - Relevance, completeness, and accuracy of source data
  - Whether conclusions are consistent with other audit evidence
- If auditor's expert's work not adequate, agree with expert on nature and extent of further work or perform additional audit procedures

**Examples of Experts:** Valuers, actuaries, engineers, geologists, IT specialists

**When It Applies:** When specialized expertise needed beyond accounting and auditing knowledge

---

## SA 700-799: Audit Conclusions and Reporting

### SA 700: Forming an Opinion and Reporting on Financial Statements

**Objective:** Form opinion on financial statements based on evaluation of audit evidence and express that opinion clearly through written report.

**Key Requirements:**
- Evaluate whether financial statements are prepared in all material respects per applicable framework
- Conclude whether reasonable assurance obtained about whether financial statements as whole are free from material misstatement
- Evaluate whether financial statements adequately disclose significant accounting policies, are appropriate, consistently applied, and appropriately disclosed
- Evaluate whether information presented achieves fair presentation
- State in report that financial statements present fairly in all material respects (or give true and fair view)
- Report shall be in writing and contain:
  - Title indicating independent auditor's report
  - Addressee
  - Introductory paragraph identifying entity and statements audited
  - Management's responsibility for financial statements
  - Auditor's responsibility
  - Opinion paragraph with opinion on financial statements
  - Other reporting responsibilities (if applicable)
  - Signature of auditor
  - Date of report (no earlier than date auditor obtained sufficient appropriate evidence)
  - Place of signature

**When It Applies:** Every audit engagement - reporting phase

---

### SA 701: Communicating Key Audit Matters in the Independent Auditor's Report

**Objective:** Determine key audit matters and communicate them in auditor's report.

**Key Requirements (for listed entities):**
- Determine key audit matters from matters communicated to those charged with governance
- Key audit matters = matters that, in auditor's professional judgment, were of most significance in audit of current period financial statements
- Consider areas of higher assessed risk or significant risks, significant auditor judgments relating to areas requiring significant management judgment, and effect of significant events/transactions
- Describe each key audit matter in separate section under heading "Key Audit Matters"
- Describe why matter considered most significant and how matter addressed in audit
- **Does not:** Replace opinion, substitute for qualified opinion, substitute for disclosures required by framework

**When It Applies:** Audits of complete sets of general purpose financial statements of listed entities

---

### SA 705: Modifications to the Opinion in the Independent Auditor's Report

**Objective:** Determine when to modify opinion and what type of modification is appropriate.

**Key Requirements:**
- Express qualified opinion when:
  - Having obtained sufficient appropriate evidence, concludes misstatements individually or in aggregate are material but not pervasive, OR
  - Unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence but concludes possible effects of undetected misstatements could be material but not pervasive
- Express adverse opinion when:
  - Having obtained sufficient appropriate evidence, concludes misstatements individually or in aggregate are material and pervasive
- Disclaim opinion when:
  - Unable to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence and concludes possible effects of undetected misstatements could be material and pervasive
- Include Basis for Modification paragraph immediately before Opinion paragraph describing matter giving rise to modification and its effects (if practicable and not adverse/disclaimer)

**Pervasive:** Effects that are not confined to specific elements, or if so confined represent or could represent substantial proportion of statements, or regarding disclosures are fundamental to users' understanding

**When It Applies:** When modification to unmodified opinion necessary

---

### SA 706: Emphasis of Matter Paragraphs and Other Matter Paragraphs

**Objective:** Determine when necessary to include Emphasis of Matter or Other Matter paragraphs and appropriate form.

**Key Requirements:**

**Emphasis of Matter:**
- If considers necessary to draw users' attention to matter appropriately presented or disclosed that is of such importance it is fundamental to users' understanding of financial statements
- Include immediately after Opinion paragraph under heading "Emphasis of Matter"
- Indicate not modifying opinion
- Common uses: Going concern material uncertainty, significant subsequent event, early application of new standard

**Other Matter:**
- If considers necessary to communicate matter other than those presented or disclosed in financial statements relevant to users' understanding of audit, auditor's responsibilities, or auditor's report
- Include immediately after Opinion paragraph (and after Emphasis of Matter if one included) under heading "Other Matter"
- Common uses: Other reporting responsibilities, restriction on distribution/use

**When It Applies:** When auditor judges such paragraphs necessary

---

### SA 710: Comparative Information

**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence about whether comparative information included in financial statements complies with framework.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine whether comparative information complies with requirements of applicable framework
- Perform audit procedures on comparative information (extent depends on whether corresponding figures or comparative financial statements)
- **Corresponding figures:** Ensure prior period amounts agree to prior year statements, accounting policies consistently applied or appropriately adjusted/disclosed, read prior year report to identify modifications
- **Comparative financial statements:** Comparative period is audited and reported on; opinion covers each period presented
- If prior period audited by predecessor, indicate in Other Matter paragraph: prior period audited by predecessor, type of opinion, reasons for modification if any, date of report
- If prior period not audited, state in Other Matter paragraph
- If becomes aware of material misstatement in prior period, determine effect on current period report

**When It Applies:** Every audit with comparative information (virtually all financial statements)

---

### SA 720: The Auditor's Responsibility in Relation to Other Information

**Objective:** Respond appropriately when documents containing audited financial statements and auditor's report include other information that could undermine credibility of statements and report.

**Key Requirements:**
- Read other information to identify material inconsistencies with audited financial statements
- If becomes aware of apparent material misstatement of fact in other information, discuss with management
- If material inconsistency or material misstatement of fact remains, take appropriate action including:
  - Requesting management to consult with qualified third party
  - Obtaining legal advice
  - Describing material inconsistency in Other Matter paragraph
  - Withholding report
  - Withdrawing from engagement
- Consider implications for auditor's report if uncorrected material misstatement exists

**Other Information Examples:** Director's report, management discussion and analysis, financial summaries, employment data, planned capital expenditures

**When It Applies:** When documents containing audited financial statements include other information

---

## SA 800-810: Special Purpose Frameworks

### SA 800: Special Considerations—Audits of Financial Statements Prepared in Accordance with Special Purpose Frameworks

**Objective:** Address special considerations when financial statements prepared per special purpose framework.

**Key Requirements:**
- Obtain understanding of purpose for which statements are prepared, intended users, and steps taken by management to determine acceptability of framework
- Evaluate whether statements adequately describe special purpose framework
- Report includes paragraph alerting users that statements prepared per special purpose framework and may not be suitable for another purpose (Emphasis of Matter paragraph)

**Special Purpose Frameworks:**
- Cash basis
- Tax basis
- Regulatory basis
- Contractual basis
- Other basis using definite set of logical criteria applied to all material items

**When It Applies:** Audits of special purpose financial statements

---

### SA 805: Special Considerations—Audits of Single Financial Statements and Specific Elements

**Objective:** Address special considerations when engaged to audit single financial statement or specific element.

**Key Requirements:**
- Determine whether audit of single statement or specific element can be performed as separate engagement or only with audit of complete financial statements
- Apply all relevant SAs
- Consider materiality in relation to single statement or element being audited
- Form opinion on single statement or element specifically
- If reporting on single statement or element and also auditing complete statements, issue separate reports and indicate in each whether other report has been issued

**Examples:** Statement of cash flows only, schedule of accounts receivable, schedule of externally managed assets

**When It Applies:** Single statement or element audits

---

### SA 810: Engagements to Report on Summary Financial Statements

**Objective:** Address auditor's responsibilities when engaged to report on summary financial statements.

**Key Requirements:**
- Accept engagement only if:
  - Auditor audited audited financial statements from which summary statements derived
  - Acceptable criteria used in preparation of summary statements
- Evaluate whether summary statements adequately disclose their summarized nature and identify audited statements
- Evaluate whether summary statements prepared per applied criteria
- Opinion states whether summary statements are consistent in all material respects with audited statements per applied criteria

**When It Applies:** Reporting on summary or condensed financial statements

## Quick Reference: Audit Evidence Reliability Hierarchy

**Most Reliable to Least Reliable:**

1. **Evidence obtained directly by auditor**
   - Physical examination/observation by auditor
   - Inspection by auditor
   - Confirmation received directly by auditor

2. **Evidence obtained from independent external sources**
   - Bank confirmations
   - Debtor confirmations
   - Legal confirmations
   - Supplier statements

3. **Evidence generated internally under strong controls**
   - System-generated reports with strong IT controls
   - Internally generated documents subject to effective control environment

4. **Documentary evidence**
   - Written evidence more reliable than oral
   - Original documents more reliable than photocopies/facsimiles
   - Evidence on paper/electronic medium/other medium

5. **Evidence generated internally under weak controls**
   - Manually prepared schedules
   - Management representations (necessary but alone insufficient)

**Application Guidance:**
- Combine evidence from different sources
- Obtain corroboration from multiple sources
- If conflicting evidence obtained, perform additional procedures
- More persuasive evidence needed for significant risks
