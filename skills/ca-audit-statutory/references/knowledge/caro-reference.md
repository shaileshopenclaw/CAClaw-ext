# Companies (Auditor's Report) Order 2020 (CARO 2020) Reference

## Overview

**Issued under:** Section 143(11) of Companies Act 2013
**Effective date:** Financial years commencing on or after 1st April 2020
**Supersedes:** CARO 2016

**Applicability:** Every company except:
- Banking company per Banking Regulation Act 1949
- Insurance company per Insurance Act 1938
- Section 8 company (non-profit)
- Private companies meeting ALL of: paid-up capital ≤ Rs. 50 lakhs, revenue ≤ Rs. 5 crores, outstanding loans/borrowings ≤ Rs. 50 lakhs at any time during financial year
- One person company

**Reporting Format:** Separate annexure to auditor's report

**General Principle:** If matter not applicable to company, state "Not Applicable." If information cannot be obtained despite best efforts, state that fact and report what is ascertainable.

## Clause 3(i): Fixed Assets

### (a) Property, Plant & Equipment - Records of Full Particulars

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether company maintaining proper records showing full particulars including quantitative details and situation of Property, Plant and Equipment (PPE).

**Verification:**
- Check fixed assets register maintained with description, location, acquisition date, cost, accumulated depreciation
- Verify quantitative details maintained (e.g., number of computers, vehicles, area of land)
- Confirm register updated for additions and disposals

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The Company has maintained proper records showing full particulars, including quantitative details and situation of Property, Plant and Equipment."

**Common Qualifications:**
- Fixed asset register not updated
- Quantitative details missing for certain asset categories
- Location/site details not recorded

---

### (b) Property, Plant & Equipment - Physical Verification Program

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether PPE have been physically verified by management at reasonable intervals; whether any material discrepancies noticed on verification and if so, properly dealt with in books.

**Verification:**
- Review management's physical verification program and coverage
- "Reasonable intervals" generally means major items verified every 3 years, entire PPE every 3-5 years depending on nature
- Check whether discrepancies between physical verification and books identified
- Verify treatment of discrepancies (adjustments made, explanations obtained)

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The Property, Plant and Equipment have been physically verified by the management during the year at reasonable intervals and no material discrepancies were noticed on such physical verification."

**Common Qualifications:**
- No physical verification conducted during year
- Physical verification program inadequate (e.g., only 10% of assets verified on rotation basis exceeding 5 years)
- Material discrepancies noted but not adjusted

---

### (c) Immovable Properties - Title Deeds

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether title deeds of all immovable properties (other than properties where company is lessee and lease agreements duly executed in favor of lessee) disclosed in financial statements are held in name of company. If not, provide details.

**Verification:**
- Obtain list of all immovable properties from fixed asset register
- Inspect title deeds (sale deeds, conveyance deeds, allotment letters)
- Verify property held in company's name
- If not in company's name, obtain explanation and details (previous owner, possession status, steps for transfer)

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The title deeds of all the immovable properties (other than properties where the Company is the lessee and the lease agreements are duly executed in favour of the lessee) disclosed in the financial statements are held in the name of the Company."

**Illustrative Wording (Qualification):**
"The title deeds of immovable properties aggregating to Rs. [X] lakhs are not held in the name of the Company but are held in the name of [previous owner/promoter]. The Company is in the process of getting the title deeds transferred in its name."

---

### (d) Revaluation of Property, Plant & Equipment or Intangible Assets

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether company revalued PPE or intangible assets or both during year; if yes, whether based on valuation by registered valuer and details.

**Verification:**
- Check if any revaluation conducted during year
- If yes, verify valuer is registered under Rule 2 of Companies (Registered Valuers and Valuation) Rules 2017
- Review valuation report for reasonableness and methodology
- Verify accounting treatment (revaluation reserve created)

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable - No Revaluation):**
"The Company has not revalued any of its Property, Plant and Equipment (including Right of Use assets) or intangible assets during the year."

**Illustrative Wording (Revaluation Occurred):**
"The Company has revalued its [asset category] during the year based on valuation by a registered valuer as defined under Rule 2 of Companies (Registered Valuers and Valuation) Rules, 2017. The increase in carrying value of Rs. [X] lakhs has been credited to revaluation reserve."

---

### (e) Proceedings Initiated or Pending for Holding Benami Property

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether proceedings initiated or pending against company for holding any benami property under Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act 1988 and rules; if yes, whether company appropriately disclosed in financial statements.

**Verification:**
- Obtain representation from management
- Review legal confirmations and litigations register
- Check for any notices under Benami Act
- Verify disclosure in financial statements if applicable

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"No proceedings have been initiated or are pending against the Company for holding any benami property under the Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988 and rules made thereunder."

## Clause 3(ii): Inventory

### (a) Physical Verification of Inventory

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether physical verification of inventory conducted at reasonable intervals by management and whether coverage and procedure are appropriate. Whether discrepancies noticed on physical verification of 10% or more in aggregate for each class of inventory properly dealt with in books.

**Verification:**
- Review inventory verification program
- Assess whether intervals are reasonable (generally quarterly for high-value, annually for others)
- Evaluate whether coverage adequate (100% annually or appropriate cycle count)
- Check verification procedures (counting, weighing, measuring)
- Calculate discrepancies as percentage of book value
- Verify adjustments made for discrepancies ≥ 10%

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The management has conducted physical verification of inventory at reasonable intervals during the year. In our opinion, the coverage and procedure of such verification by the management is appropriate. The discrepancies noticed on physical verification of inventory as compared to books of account were not material and have been properly dealt with in the books of account."

**Common Qualifications:**
- No physical verification conducted
- Discrepancies of 15% in raw materials not adjusted
- Verification only once a year for high-value items

---

### (b) Inventory Held by Third Parties

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether company granted working capital limits in excess of Rs. 5 crores in aggregate from banks or financial institutions based on security of current assets; if yes, whether quarterly returns/statements filed with such banks/institutions are in agreement with books (specify nature and amount of differences if material).

**Verification:**
- Obtain list of working capital facilities and limits sanctioned
- If aggregate limits > Rs. 5 crores based on current assets, review quarterly stock statements submitted
- Reconcile stock statements with books for same period
- Identify material differences (generally > 5% or > Rs. 25 lakhs)
- Obtain explanations for differences

**Illustrative Wording (Not Applicable):**
"The Company has not been sanctioned any working capital limits in excess of five crore rupees in aggregate from banks or financial institutions during the year on the basis of security of current assets."

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The quarterly returns/statements filed by the Company with such banks or financial institutions are in agreement with the books of account of the Company."

**Illustrative Wording (Qualification):**
"The quarterly stock statements filed by the Company with banks for the quarter ended [date] show inventory of Rs. [X] lakhs as against book balance of Rs. [Y] lakhs, resulting in a difference of Rs. [Z] lakhs. This difference is on account of [reason]."

## Clause 3(iii): Investments, Guarantees, Security, and Loans

### (a) Compliance with Section 185 (Loans to Directors) and Section 186 (Investments/Loans/Guarantees)

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether provisions of Sections 185 and 186 complied with in respect of investments made, guarantees provided, security given, and loans granted.

**Verification:**

**Section 185 - Loans to Directors:**
- Check if any loans/guarantees/security provided to directors or related parties
- Verify exceptions: loan to managing/whole-time director as per remuneration policy, loan to company where director holds < 2% shares
- If covered, ensure compliance impossible (generally prohibited)

**Section 186 - Investments/Loans/Guarantees/Security:**
- Aggregate all investments, loans, guarantees, security given
- Verify not exceeding limits (generally 60% of paid-up capital + free reserves + securities premium, or 100% of free reserves + securities premium, whichever higher)
- Check Board approval obtained
- Verify disclosure in financial statements

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"In our opinion, the provisions of Section 185 and 186 of the Companies Act, 2013 in respect of loans and investments made, guarantees given and securities provided have been complied with by the Company."

**Common Qualifications:**
- Loan to director not covered under exemptions
- Aggregate investments/loans exceeded limits under Section 186
- Board resolution not obtained for guarantee

---

### (b) Terms and Conditions of Loans/Advances

**Reporting Requirement:** In respect of investments and loans, whether terms and conditions are prima facie prejudicial to company's interest.

**Verification:**
- Review loan agreements for interest rate, repayment terms, security
- Compare interest rates with market rates or company's borrowing cost
- Assess whether unsecured loans to related parties at below-market rates
- Evaluate business rationale

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"In our opinion, the investments made, guarantees provided, security given and the terms and conditions of the grant of loans and advances in the nature of loans are not prima facie prejudicial to the company's interest."

---

### (c) Schedule of Repayment of Principal and Interest - Regularity

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether schedule of repayment of principal and payment of interest stipulated and whether repayments/receipts are regular.

**Verification:**
- Review loan agreements for repayment schedule
- Check actual receipts against due dates
- Identify overdue amounts and delays
- Obtain explanations for delays

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"In respect of loans granted by the Company, the schedule of repayment of principal and payment of interest has been stipulated and the repayments or receipts of principal and interest are regular as per stipulation."

---

### (d) Overdue Amounts > Rs. 1 Crore - Steps for Recovery

**Reporting Requirement:** If amount overdue for more than 90 days aggregates to Rs. 1 crore or more, whether reasonable steps taken for recovery.

**Verification:**
- Identify loans/advances overdue > 90 days
- If aggregate ≥ Rs. 1 crore, review recovery steps (reminder letters, legal notice, arbitration, suit filed)
- Assess adequacy of steps

**Illustrative Wording (Not Applicable):**
"There are no amounts of loans granted by the Company which are overdue for more than ninety days."

**Illustrative Wording (Qualification):**
"Loan of Rs. [X] crores granted to [Party Name] is overdue for more than 90 days. The Company has issued legal notice but has not initiated recovery proceedings."

---

### (e) Loans Granted Falling Due - Renewal/Repayment

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether any loan granted falling due during year has been renewed or extended or fresh loans granted to settle overdue; if yes, aggregate amount and percentage to total loans.

**Verification:**
- Identify loans falling due during year
- Check if renewed, extended, or fresh loan granted to settle overdue (evergreening)
- Calculate aggregate amount and as percentage of total loans granted

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The Company has not granted any loans or advances in the nature of loans which have fallen due during the year that have been renewed or extended or fresh loans granted to settle the overdue of existing loans given to the same parties."

**Illustrative Wording (Qualification):**
"The Company has granted fresh loans aggregating to Rs. [X] lakhs to [Party Name] to settle the overdue existing loans of Rs. [Y] lakhs, which represents [Z]% of total loans granted during the year."

---

### (f) Loans Granted Repayable on Demand or Without Specifying Terms

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether any loans granted either repayable on demand or without specifying any terms or period of repayment; if yes, aggregate amount and percentage to total loans.

**Verification:**
- Review loan agreements for repayment terms
- Identify loans "repayable on demand" or with no specified terms/timeline
- Calculate aggregate and percentage

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The Company has not granted any loans or advances in the nature of loans either repayable on demand or without specifying any terms or period of repayment."

**Illustrative Wording (Qualification):**
"The Company has granted loans aggregating to Rs. [X] crores which are repayable on demand. This represents [Y]% of the total loans granted as at balance sheet date."

## Clause 3(v): Deposits

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether company accepted any deposits or amounts deemed to be deposits; if yes, whether directives issued by Reserve Bank of India, provisions of Sections 73 to 76 or any other relevant provisions of Act and rules complied with. If not, nature of contraventions to be stated. If order passed by Company Law Board/National Company Law Tribunal/Reserve Bank of India or any court or any other tribunal, whether complied with.

**Verification:**
- Identify any deposits accepted from public or shareholders
- Check compliance with Section 73 (acceptance limits, credit rating, deposit insurance)
- Verify whether deemed deposits exist (unsecured loans from public, directors beyond limits)
- Review exemptions (from directors/relatives, group companies)
- Check filing of Form DPT-3 annually

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The Company has not accepted any deposits from the public within the meaning of Sections 73 to 76 of the Act and the rules framed thereunder to the extent notified."

**Common Qualifications:**
- Unsecured loans from public classified as deemed deposits - compliance not ensured
- Deposit acceptance limit exceeded
- Form DPT-3 not filed

## Clause 3(vii): Statutory Dues

### (a) Regular Deposit of Undisputed Statutory Dues

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether company regular in depositing undisputed statutory dues including Goods and Services Tax, Provident Fund, Employees State Insurance, Income-tax, Sales Tax, Service Tax, duty of Customs, duty of Excise, Value Added Tax, cess and any other statutory dues with appropriate authorities. If not regular, extent of arrears to be stated.

**Verification:**

- Check payment challans and dates

## Clause 3(xiii): Related Party Transactions

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether all transactions with related parties are in compliance with Sections 177 and 188 where applicable; if not, details of non-compliance. Whether details of such transactions disclosed in financial statements as required by applicable accounting standards.

**Verification:**

**Section 188:** Approval for related party transactions
- Identify related party transactions entered during year
- Verify Board approval obtained (for contracts beyond ordinary course or not at arm's length)
- Check special resolution in general meeting obtained where required (transactions exceeding thresholds)

**Accounting Standards Disclosure:**
- Verify compliance with AS 18 (Ind AS 24) disclosure requirements

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"In our opinion, all transactions with the related parties are in compliance with Sections 177 and 188 of Companies Act, 2013 where applicable and the details have been disclosed in the Financial Statements as required by the applicable accounting standards."

## Clause 3(xx): CSR Expenditure

### (a) CSR Amount to be Spent During Year - Whether Spent

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether company having net worth ≥ Rs. 500 crores or turnover ≥ Rs. 1000 crores or net profit ≥ Rs. 5 crores has spent required amount on CSR activities; if not, reasons for not spending.

**Verification:**
- Check whether company covered under Section 135 thresholds (met in current + 2 immediately preceding years)
- Calculate 2% of average net profits of preceding 3 years
- Verify actual CSR expenditure during year
- Check whether unspent amount transferred to specified fund or Unspent CSR Account

**Illustrative Wording (Not Applicable):**
"The Company does not fall within the criteria mentioned under Section 135(1) of the Companies Act, 2013 and accordingly reporting under clause (xx) is not applicable."

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"The Company has spent the required amount on Corporate Social Responsibility activities during the year."

**Illustrative Wording (Shortfall):**
"The Company was required to spend Rs. [X] lakhs towards CSR activities during the year. However, the Company has spent only Rs. [Y] lakhs. The unspent amount of Rs. [Z] lakhs has been transferred to Unspent CSR Account within the stipulated time."

### (b) Unspent CSR Amount from Ongoing Projects - Transfer to Special Account

**Reporting Requirement:** Whether there is any unspent amount from ongoing CSR projects; if yes, whether transferred to special account per Section 135(6).

**Verification:**
- Identify ongoing CSR projects as per CSR policy
- Calculate unspent amount on ongoing projects
- Verify transfer to Unspent CSR Account within 30 days of financial year end
- Check whether utilized from special account within 3 years, else transferred to specified fund

**Illustrative Wording (Favorable):**
"There is no unspent amount towards Corporate Social Responsibility requiring a transfer to special account in compliance with the provisions of Section 135(6) of the Companies Act, 2013."
