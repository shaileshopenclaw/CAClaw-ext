# Audit Methodology Reference

This skill provides comprehensive guidance on statutory audit methodology following Indian Standards on Auditing (SAs), covering the complete engagement lifecycle, assertion framework, risk-based approach, and documentation standards.

## 1. Statutory Audit Engagement Lifecycle

### Phase 1: Pre-Engagement
**Objective:** Assess whether to accept/continue the engagement

**Key Activities:**
- Client acceptance/continuance evaluation (SA 220)
- Independence and ethics assessment (Code of Ethics)
- Engagement team competence evaluation
- Preliminary engagement activities
- Terms of engagement documentation (SA 210)

**Outputs:**
- Engagement letter signed by client
- Independence declarations from audit team
- Client acceptance checklist completed

### Phase 2: Planning
**Objective:** Develop audit strategy and detailed audit plan

**Key Activities:**
- Understand the entity and its environment (SA 315)
- Assess risks of material misstatement (inherent and control risks)
- Determine materiality levels (SA 320)
- Design overall audit strategy
- Develop detailed audit plan with specific procedures
- Plan for specialists, other auditors (SA 620, SA 600)

**Outputs:**
- Overall audit strategy memorandum
- Detailed audit plan by area/assertion
- Materiality calculations and rationale
- Risk assessment documentation
- Planned audit procedures matrix

### Phase 3: Execution
**Objective:** Obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence

**Key Activities:**
- Test of controls (if control risk assessed below high)
- Substantive procedures (tests of details and analytical procedures)
- External confirmations (SA 505)
- Analytical procedures (SA 520)
- Audit sampling (SA 530)
- Related party procedures (SA 550)
- Subsequent events review (SA 560)
- Going concern assessment (SA 570)

**Outputs:**
- Working papers documenting all procedures performed
- Audit evidence obtained and evaluated
- Differences/adjustments schedule
- Management representation points identified

### Phase 4: Completion
**Objective:** Finalize audit work and form opinion

**Key Activities:**
- Overall analytical procedures review
- Evaluate audit evidence sufficiency and appropriateness
- Evaluate uncorrected misstatements (SA 450)
- Obtain management representations (SA 580)
- Subsequent events review through report date
- Going concern final evaluation
- Review of other information (SA 720)
- Engagement quality control review (SA 220)

**Outputs:**
- Summary of uncorrected misstatements
- Management representation letter
- Audit completion checklist
- Draft financial statements reviewed
- Audit opinion formulated

### Phase 5: Reporting
**Objective:** Communicate audit findings and issue opinion

**Key Activities:**
- Draft audit report per SA 700/701/705/706
- Communicate with those charged with governance (SA 260)
- Issue final audit report
- Archive audit files (SA 230)

**Outputs:**
- Independent Auditor's Report
- Communication to those charged with governance
- Archived and signed-off audit file

## 2. Assertion Framework

### Transaction-Level Assertions (SA 315)

**Occurrence**
- Definition: Transactions and events that have been recorded have occurred and pertain to the entity
- Example tests: Vouch sales invoices to delivery challans and customer orders

**Completeness**
- Definition: All transactions and events that should have been recorded have been recorded
- Example tests: Trace from goods dispatch register to sales recorded in books

**Accuracy**
- Definition: Amounts and other data relating to transactions have been recorded appropriately
- Example tests: Recalculate invoice amounts, verify pricing against approved rate lists

**Cutoff**
- Definition: Transactions have been recorded in the correct accounting period
- Example tests: Examine transactions around year-end for proper period classification

**Classification**
- Definition: Transactions have been recorded in the proper accounts
- Example tests: Review journal entries for appropriate account coding

### Balance-Level Assertions (Account Balances)

**Existence**
- Definition: Assets, liabilities, and equity interests exist
- Example tests: Physical verification of inventory, confirmation of receivables

**Rights and Obligations**
- Definition: The entity holds or controls rights to assets and liabilities are obligations of the entity
- Example tests: Review title deeds for property, examine loan agreements for obligations

**Completeness**
- Definition: All assets, liabilities and equity that should have been recorded have been recorded
- Example tests: Search for unrecorded liabilities, review post-year-end payments

**Valuation and Allocation**
- Definition: Assets, liabilities and equity are included in financial statements at appropriate amounts
- Example tests: Test NRV of inventory, assess allowance for doubtful debts

### Presentation and Disclosure Assertions

**Occurrence and Rights and Obligations**
- Definition: Disclosed events and transactions have occurred and pertain to the entity
- Example tests: Verify contingent liabilities actually exist

**Completeness**
- Definition: All required disclosures have been included
- Example tests: Check Ind AS/Schedule III compliance checklist

**Classification and Understandability**
- Definition: Financial information is appropriately presented and disclosures are clearly expressed
- Example tests: Review classification of current vs non-current items

**Accuracy and Valuation**
- Definition: Information is disclosed fairly and at appropriate amounts
- Example tests: Agree disclosed amounts to accounting records

## 3. Risk-Based Audit Approach

### Audit Risk Model (SA 200)

**Formula:** AR = IR × CR × DR

Where:
- **AR (Audit Risk):** Risk that auditor expresses inappropriate opinion when financial statements are materially misstated
- **IR (Inherent Risk):** Susceptibility of an assertion to material misstatement, before consideration of controls
- **CR (Control Risk):** Risk that misstatement will not be prevented/detected by entity's internal controls
- **DR (Detection Risk):** Risk that auditor's procedures will not detect material misstatement

### Risk Assessment Process

**Step 1: Understand the Entity**
- Industry, regulatory, and other external factors
- Nature of entity, ownership structure
- Accounting policies and financial reporting practices
- Objectives, strategies, and related business risks
- Internal control system

**Step 2: Identify Risks**
- Fraud risks (SA 240)
- Significant risks requiring special audit consideration
- Risks related to significant accounting estimates
- Routine processing risks

**Step 3: Assess Risk Levels**
- Rate inherent risk (Low/Medium/High)
- Assess control design and implementation
- Rate control risk (Low/Medium/High)
- Document risk assessment rationale

**Step 4: Design Responsive Procedures**
- Higher risk → More substantive testing
- Lower risk (with effective controls) → May test controls and reduce substantive work
- Determine nature, timing, extent of procedures

## 4. Audit Evidence Hierarchy

### Reliability Ranking (Most to Least Reliable)

1. **Auditor's direct knowledge**
   - Physical inspection
   - Observation
   - Recalculation/reperformance

2. **External evidence obtained directly by auditor**
   - Bank confirmations
   - Debtor/creditor confirmations
   - Third-party confirmations

3. **External evidence obtained via client**
   - Supplier invoices
   - Bank statements
   - Third-party documents

4. **Internal evidence with strong controls**
   - Purchase orders (with proper authorization)
   - Goods received notes
   - Internal vouchers under good control environment

5. **Internal evidence without strong controls**
   - Management representations
   - Internally generated schedules
   - Oral explanations

### Sufficiency and Appropriateness (SA 500)

**Sufficiency:** Measure of quantity of audit evidence
- Affected by risk assessment and quality of evidence
- Higher risk → More evidence needed

**Appropriateness:** Measure of quality (relevance and reliability)
- Affected by source and nature of evidence
- External evidence generally more reliable than internal

## 5. Working Paper Standards (SA 230)

### Documentation Requirements

**Mandatory Documentation:**
- Audit procedures performed
- Relevant evidence obtained
- Conclusions reached
- Significant professional judgments
- Who performed work and date
- Who reviewed work and date

**Specific Requirements:**
- Discussions of significant matters with management/governance
- Identified and assessed risks
- Materiality levels and basis
- Nature, timing, and extent of procedures
- Results of procedures and evidence obtained
- How inconsistencies were resolved

### File Assembly
- Complete within 60 days of report date
- No deletion after assembly; additions must be documented
- Retain for minimum period per regulatory requirements (typically 7-10 years in India)

## 6. Materiality Framework (SA 320)

### Materiality Levels

**Overall Materiality (OM)**
- Used to determine scope of audit
- Benchmark examples:
  - Profit-oriented: 5% of profit before tax (3-10% range)
  - Revenue-oriented: 0.5-1% of revenue
  - Asset-based: 1-2% of total assets
  - Not-for-profit: % of total expenses/revenue

**Performance Materiality (PM)**
- Set below OM to reduce risk that aggregate uncorrected misstatements exceed OM
- Typically 50-75% of OM depending on risk assessment

**Specific Materiality**
- For particular classes of transactions, balances, or disclosures
- Example: Related party transactions, directors' remuneration

**Clearly Trivial Threshold**
- Below which misstatements need not be accumulated
- Typically 3-5% of OM

### Revision During Audit
- Revise if circumstances change (e.g., actual results differ significantly from budgeted)
- Document rationale for revision

## 7. Audit Sampling (SA 530)

### Sampling Approaches

**Statistical Sampling**
- Random selection
- Sample size determined mathematically
- Results can be statistically projected
- Methods: Random sampling, systematic sampling, monetary unit sampling (MUS)

**Non-Statistical Sampling**
- Professional judgment used for selection
- Judgmental sample size determination
- Results projected based on auditor's judgment
- Methods: Haphazard selection, block selection

### Sample Size Factors
- Tolerable error (lower → larger sample)
- Expected error (higher → larger sample)
- Confidence level required (higher → larger sample)
- Population size (generally minor effect)

### Evaluation of Results
- Project sample findings to population
- Compare to tolerable error
- Consider qualitative aspects of deviations
- Document conclusions and basis

## 8. Key Professional Judgments

### Areas Requiring Significant Judgment
- Going concern assessment
- Accounting estimates evaluation (fair value, provisions, ECL)
- Revenue recognition timing and measurement
- Fraud risk assessment and response
- Related party identification and disclosure
- Subsequent events evaluation
- Group audit considerations

### Documentation of Judgments
- Facts and circumstances considered
- Alternative treatments considered
- Basis for conclusion reached
- Discussions with specialists/management
- Resolution of conflicting evidence

## 9. Quality Control Considerations (SA 220)

### Engagement Partner Responsibilities
- Leadership responsibility for quality
- Relevant ethical requirements compliance
- Engagement team competence and capability
- Audit performance per SAs
- Appropriate engagement resources

### Engagement Quality Control Review
- Required for listed entities and high-risk engagements
- Objective evaluation of significant judgments
- Review of financial statements and proposed report
- Performed before report issuance
- Documented conclusions

## 10. Common Audit Approach by Area

### Revenue
- Assertions: Occurrence, Completeness, Accuracy, Cutoff
- Procedures: Analytical review, invoice-to-delivery testing, cutoff testing, confirmation

### Receivables
- Assertions: Existence, Valuation, Rights
- Procedures: Confirmations, aging analysis, subsequent collections, provision adequacy

### Inventory
- Assertions: Existence, Valuation, Rights
- Procedures: Physical observation, NRV testing, cutoff, cost buildup verification

### Property, Plant & Equipment
- Assertions: Existence, Rights, Valuation
- Procedures: Physical verification, title deed review, depreciation recalculation, additions/disposals vouching

### Payables
- Assertions: Completeness, Existence, Accuracy
- Procedures: Confirmations, search for unrecorded liabilities, reconciliations, subsequent payments

### Expenses
- Assertions: Occurrence, Completeness, Classification, Cutoff
- Procedures: Analytical review, vouching to supporting documents, cutoff testing
