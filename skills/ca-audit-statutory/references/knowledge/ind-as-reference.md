# Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS) - Audit Reference

This skill provides key provisions of Indian Accounting Standards relevant to statutory audit judgments, focusing on areas requiring significant auditor attention and professional skepticism.

## 1. Ind AS 36 - Impairment of Assets

### Scope and Key Concepts

**Applicability:** All assets except inventories, deferred tax, financial assets, investment property at fair value, biological assets, insurance contract assets

**Impairment Indicators (External):**
- Market value decline significantly more than expected
- Significant adverse changes in technology, market, economy, legal environment
- Market interest rates increased affecting discount rate
- Carrying amount exceeds market capitalization

**Impairment Indicators (Internal):**
- Evidence of obsolescence or physical damage
- Significant changes in asset use (idle, restructuring, early disposal plans)
- Asset economic performance worse than expected

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Recoverable Amount Determination:**
- Higher of: Fair Value Less Costs of Disposal (FVLCD) or Value in Use (VIU)
- Challenge management's cash flow projections
- Assess discount rate appropriateness (WACC components)
- Evaluate terminal value/growth rate assumptions
- Test sensitivity to key assumptions

**Cash Generating Units (CGUs):**
- Verify CGU identification is appropriate (smallest identifiable group generating independent cash inflows)
- Check goodwill allocation to CGUs is reasonable
- Ensure corporate assets allocated on reasonable basis

**Reversal of Impairment:**
- Permitted for all assets except goodwill
- Limited to amount that would have been determined if no impairment recognized
- Verify reversal indicators exist and calculation is correct

**Common Audit Issues:**
- Over-optimistic cash flow projections
- Discount rates not reflecting asset-specific risks
- Lack of consistency between budget and actual performance
- CGU definition too aggregated to mask underperforming assets

## 2. Ind AS 37 - Provisions, Contingent Liabilities and Contingent Assets

### Recognition Criteria for Provisions

Must meet ALL three criteria:
1. Present obligation (legal or constructive) from past event
2. Probable outflow of economic benefits to settle
3. Reliable estimate can be made

### Key Definitions

**Provision:** Liability of uncertain timing or amount
**Contingent Liability:**
- Possible obligation dependent on uncertain future events, OR
- Present obligation not recognized because outflow not probable or amount cannot be reliably estimated

**Contingent Asset:** Possible asset dependent on uncertain future events

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Provision Recognition:**
- Verify past obligating event exists
- Assess probability of outflow (>50% = probable)
- Challenge basis of estimation (expected value or most likely outcome)
- Ensure discount to present value if time value material

**Common Provisions to Audit:**
- Warranty obligations (historical claim rates, current defect trends)
- Litigation provisions (legal assessment, similar case outcomes)
- Onerous contracts (unavoidable costs exceed expected benefits)
- Decommissioning/restoration obligations (technical estimates, discount rate)
- Restructuring (detailed formal plan, valid expectation created)

**Contingent Liability Disclosure:**
- Verify all material contingencies disclosed
- Assess possibility vs probability classification
- Remote contingencies need not be disclosed
- Common areas: tax disputes, legal claims, guarantees

**Specific Audit Procedures:**
- Obtain lawyer's letters for litigation matters
- Review board minutes for obligations created
- Analyze subsequent payments against provisions
- Test historical accuracy of estimates vs actuals

## 3. Ind AS 109 - Financial Instruments

### Classification and Measurement

**Financial Assets Classification:**
- **Amortized Cost:** Business model = hold to collect, cash flows are SPPI (solely payments of principal and interest)
- **FVOCI:** Business model = hold to collect AND sell, cash flows are SPPI
- **FVTPL:** All others (default category)

**Financial Liabilities:**
- Generally at amortized cost
- FVTPL if held for trading or designated

### Expected Credit Loss (ECL) Model

**Three-Stage Approach:**

**Stage 1 (Performing):**
- 12-month ECL
- Credit risk not increased significantly since initial recognition

**Stage 2 (Underperforming):**
- Lifetime ECL
- Significant increase in credit risk (SICR) but not credit-impaired

**Stage 3 (Non-performing):**
- Lifetime ECL
- Credit-impaired (objective evidence of impairment event)

### Auditor Focus Areas

**ECL Model Audit:**
- Assess methodology appropriateness (individual vs collective, historical loss rates vs probability of default)
- Test SICR criteria (30+ days past due rebuttable presumption)
- Verify forward-looking information incorporated (economic scenarios, weightings)
- Challenge recovery rate assumptions for Stage 3 assets
- Test completeness of exposures subject to ECL

**Business Model Assessment:**
- Verify entity's stated business model matches actual behavior
- Test frequency and significance of sales from "hold to collect" portfolios
- Assess whether sales due to credit deterioration only

**SPPI Test:**
- Review contractual terms for leverage, non-recourse, contingent features
- Identify any modified time value of money characteristics

**Common Audit Issues:**
- Inadequate SICR criteria leading to understated Stage 2 assets
- Over-optimistic recovery rates for NPAs
- Insufficient forward-looking adjustment
- Trade receivable ECL using simplified approach without adequate loss rate support

## 4. Ind AS 115 - Revenue from Contracts with Customers

### Five-Step Model

1. Identify the contract with customer
2. Identify performance obligations
3. Determine transaction price
4. Allocate transaction price to performance obligations
5. Recognize revenue when (or as) performance obligation satisfied

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Performance Obligation Identification:**
- Verify each promised good/service is distinct
- Challenge bundling/unbundling of deliverables
- Assess principal vs agent considerations

**Transaction Price:**
- Variable consideration: verify constraint applied (only to extent highly probable no significant reversal)
- Significant financing component: check if adjustment made (>12 months)
- Non-cash consideration: verify fair value measurement
- Consideration payable to customer: verify treated as reduction of revenue

**Satisfaction of Performance Obligation:**
- Over time recognition: verify criteria met (customer controls asset as created, OR no alternative use + enforceable right to payment)
- Point in time: verify control transfer indicators (legal title, physical possession, payment, acceptance)
- Measure progress appropriately (input or output methods)

**Contract Modifications:**
- Assess whether modification creates new contract or modifies existing
- Verify accounting treatment consistent with modification type

**Common Audit Issues:**
- Revenue recognized before performance obligation satisfied
- Variable consideration not constrained adequately (revenue overstatement risk)
- Inappropriate over-time recognition (lacks enforceable payment right)
- Contract assets vs receivables misclassification

## 5. Ind AS 116 - Leases

### Lessee Accounting

**Recognition:** Right-of-use (ROU) asset and lease liability for all leases except:
- Short-term leases (≤12 months)
- Low-value asset leases

**Initial Measurement:**
- Lease liability: PV of lease payments using incremental borrowing rate (or rate implicit in lease)
- ROU asset: Lease liability + initial direct costs + prepayments - lease incentives

**Subsequent Measurement:**
- Lease liability: Amortized cost using effective interest method
- ROU asset: Cost model (cost less accumulated depreciation and impairment)

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Lease Identification:**
- Verify all contracts assessed for embedded leases
- Check if contract conveys right to control identified asset (obtain substantially all economic benefits + direct use)
- Service contracts often contain embedded leases (IT equipment, vehicles, warehousing space)

**Discount Rate:**
- Verify incremental borrowing rate derivation (risk-free rate + credit spread + asset/lease-specific adjustment)
- Assess reasonableness relative to entity's actual borrowing rates

**Lease Payments:**
- Include: Fixed payments, variable payments based on index/rate, exercise price of purchase option if reasonably certain, termination penalties
- Verify variable payments based on performance/usage excluded from liability (expensed as incurred)

**Lease Term:**
- Challenge assessment of extension/termination option exercise likelihood
- Consider economic incentives, past practice, business needs

**Common Audit Issues:**
- Incomplete identification of leases (embedded leases in service contracts)
- Inappropriately high discount rates (understating liability/ROU)
- Lease term too short (ignoring likely extensions)
- Variable rent misclassified as fixed

## 6. Ind AS 12 - Income Taxes

### Key Concepts

**Current Tax:** Tax payable/recoverable on taxable profit for period
**Deferred Tax:** Tax attributable to temporary differences and unused tax losses/credits

**Temporary Differences:**
- **Taxable temporary differences:** Result in taxable amounts in future (create DTL)
- **Deductible temporary differences:** Result in deductible amounts in future (create DTA)

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Current Tax:**
- Verify tax computation reconciles to book profit
- Test major book-tax differences (permanent and temporary)
- Verify tax rates applied are correct
- Review tax provision adequacy vs actual assessments

**Deferred Tax:**
- Recalculate deferred tax on major temporary differences
- Verify tax base vs carrying amount computations
- Assess DTA recognition probability (convincing evidence of sufficient future taxable profit)
- Test DTA reversal pattern assumptions
- Verify tax rate used is enacted/substantively enacted rate expected at reversal

**Common Items Creating Temporary Differences:**
- Depreciation (tax vs book rates differ)
- Provisions not allowable until paid (warranty, doubtful debts, employee benefits)
- Carried forward losses/unabsorbed depreciation
- Revenue recognition timing differences
- Financial instruments fair value changes

**Disclosures:**
- Effective tax rate reconciliation
- DTA/DTL major components
- Unrecognized DTA (with reasons)

## 7. Ind AS 8 - Accounting Policies, Changes in Accounting Estimates and Errors

### Key Distinctions

**Change in Accounting Policy:**
- Rare; only if required by standard or results in more relevant/reliable information
- Retrospective application (restate comparatives)

**Change in Accounting Estimate:**
- Common; due to new information or developments
- Prospective application (current and future periods only)

**Prior Period Error:**
- Omission or misstatement in prior period statements
- Retrospective restatement (if material)

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Change in Estimate vs Policy:**
- Verify entity's classification is appropriate
- Common estimates changing: useful lives, residual values, ECL rates, fair values, warranty rates
- Policy changes require strong justification

**Retrospective Adjustments:**
- Verify opening retained earnings adjusted correctly
- Check comparative figures restated
- Assess disclosure adequacy (nature, amount, reason for change)

**Materiality of Errors:**
- Assess whether prior period error material (quantitative and qualitative)
- If immaterial, correction in current period is acceptable

## 8. Ind AS 10 - Events After Reporting Period

### Classification

**Adjusting Events:** Provide evidence of conditions that existed at reporting date
- Examples: Settlement of court case, bankruptcy of customer, sale of inventory below cost

**Non-Adjusting Events:** Indicative of conditions that arose after reporting date
- Examples: Major business combination, fire/natural disaster, decline in market value of investments

### Auditor Focus Areas

**Cut-off Date:** Date when financial statements authorized for issue (by Board/management as applicable)

**Adjusting Event Procedures:**
- Verify adjustment reflects condition at year-end (not new information)
- Recalculate impact on financial statements
- Check disclosure if additional information needed

**Non-Adjusting Events:**
- Material events require disclosure (nature and financial impact estimate)
- Going concern implications must be assessed

**Dividends:**
- Proposed dividend after reporting period = non-adjusting (disclose, don't provide)
- Only provide if legally obligated at reporting date

**Common Procedures:**
- Review board minutes from year-end through audit report date
- Review management accounts and forecasts
- Inquire about litigation, commitments, contingencies
- Search for unrecorded liabilities via post-year-end payments
