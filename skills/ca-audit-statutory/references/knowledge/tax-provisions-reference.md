# Income Tax Act 1961 - Tax Audit Reference

This skill provides key provisions of the Income Tax Act 1961 relevant to tax audit under Section 44AB, focusing on areas requiring auditor reporting and common compliance issues.

## 1. Section 44AB - Tax Audit Applicability

### Mandatory Tax Audit Thresholds

**Business (Non-Presumptive):**
- Total sales/turnover/gross receipts > Rs. 1 crore in previous year

**Business (Presumptive u/s 44AD):**
- Total sales/turnover/gross receipts > Rs. 2 crore, AND
- Income not declared u/s 44AD (i.e., claims lower profit than presumptive 8%/6%)
- Note: If declared u/s 44AD (even > Rs. 2 crore), no audit required

**Business (Presumptive u/s 44ADA - Professionals):**
- Gross receipts > Rs. 50 lakh, AND
- Income not declared u/s 44ADA (i.e., claims lower than presumptive 50%)

**Profession:**
- Gross receipts > Rs. 50 lakh in previous year

**Cash Transactions (Special Threshold):**
- Even if turnover < Rs. 1 crore, audit required if:
  - Total cash receipts > Rs. 1 crore, OR
  - Total cash payments > Rs. 1 crore
- "Cash" includes both physical cash and digital payments not via banking channels

### Due Date
- September 30 (if no transfer pricing report required)
- October 31 (if transfer pricing report required under Section 92E)

## 2. Section 40A(2) - Excessive Payments to Related Parties

### Scope
Disallowance of excessive or unreasonable payments to related parties if payment exceeds fair market value.

### Related Parties Include
- Relative (as defined)
- Director or substantial shareholder and their relatives
- Firm/AOP and its members
- Entities where substantial interest exists

### Auditor Responsibilities
- Identify related party transactions per Form 3CD Clause 18
- Assess reasonableness of payments (salaries, rent, interest, professional fees)
- Report transactions where payment appears excessive vs market rates
- Common areas of scrutiny:
  - Director remuneration exceeding Schedule V limits
  - Interest rates above market rates
  - Rent significantly above prevailing rates
  - Management fees without proper justification

### Consequences
Excess amount disallowed in computing business income.

## 3. Section 40A(3) - Cash Payments Exceeding Threshold

### Rule: Disallowance for Cash Payments ≥ Rs. 10,000

**Applicability:**
- Payment to a single person in a single day ≥ Rs. 10,000 made in cash
- 100% of payment disallowed (entire amount, not just excess)

**Exceptions (as per Rule 6DD):**
- Payments to government, banking company, cooperative bank
- Force majeure circumstances
- Payments in remote areas without banking facilities
- Other specified exceptions (RBI restrictions, post office payments, etc.)

### Auditor Responsibilities
- Report cash payments exceeding Rs. 10,000 in Form 3CD Clause 26
- Verify if exceptions apply
- Test sample of expense vouchers for cash payment mode
- Flag systemic cash payment practices

**Common Issues:**
- Multiple cash payments just below Rs. 10,000 to same party (splitting)
- Inadequate documentation of force majeure claims
- Improper exception claims

## 4. Section 43B - Payment-Based Deductions

### Specified Expenses Deductible Only When Actually Paid

**Covered Items:**
- Any tax, duty, cess, or fee (by whatever name) to government
- Employer's contribution to PF, ESI, superannuation, or other employee welfare funds
- Bonus or commission to employees
- Interest on loans from public financial institutions, scheduled banks, state financial corporations
- Land revenue (leave & license fees - added by Finance Act 2023)

**Payment Deadline:**
- Must be paid before due date of filing income tax return u/s 139(1)
- If paid after year-end but before return due date: Allowed in that year
- If paid after return due date: Allowed in year of payment

### Exception for Statutory Dues
- For statutory dues (PF, ESI), payment deadline is "actual due date" (not grace period)
- Example: PF for March due on April 15; if paid on April 20, disallowed even if return not yet due

### Auditor Responsibilities
- Report in Form 3CD Clause 23: Amount payable as per Act/Rule but not paid before due date
- Common items to check:
  - PF contributions (ECR filing vs payment proof)
  - ESI contributions
  - GST payments
  - Professional tax
  - Bonus provisions vs actual payment
  - Interest accrued vs paid

**Common Issues:**
- PF/ESI paid after due date but before return date (still disallowed)
- Provisions created for bonus/commission not paid before return due date
- Interest booked but not paid

## 5. Section 269SS - Cash Loans/Deposits Exceeding Rs. 20,000

### Prohibition
No person shall take or accept any loan or deposit ≥ Rs. 20,000 otherwise than by account payee cheque/draft or ECS.

**Exceptions:**
- Government, banking company, corporation established by statute
- Deposit with primary agricultural society/co-op society
- Other specified entities

### Consequences
- Penalty: 100% of amount (not applicable if reasonable cause shown)
- Not disallowance of interest/principal repayment in computing income

### Auditor Responsibility
Report in Form 3CD Clause 31: Amount of loan/deposit taken/accepted in contravention.

## 6. Section 269ST - Cash Receipt Exceeding Rs. 2 Lakh

### Prohibition (Effective from April 1, 2017)
No person shall receive ≥ Rs. 2 lakh in cash:
- In aggregate from a single person in a day, OR
- In respect of a single transaction, OR
- In respect of transactions relating to one event/occasion

**"Cash" Definition:** Includes coins, currency notes, demand drafts, and pay orders (but NOT account payee cheques/drafts).

### Consequences
- Penalty: 100% of amount received (on recipient)
- Not disallowance of receipt/revenue

### Auditor Responsibility
Report in Form 3CD Clause 31(a): Whether cash receipts exceed Rs. 2 lakh in contravention.

**Common Violations:**
- Cash sales to single customer across multiple invoices in a day
- Event-related receipts (weddings, conferences) in cash
- Advance receipts in cash for single transaction

## 7. Section 269T - Cash Repayment of Loans/Deposits Exceeding Rs. 20,000

### Prohibition
No person shall repay any loan or deposit ≥ Rs. 20,000 otherwise than by account payee cheque/draft or ECS.

**Exceptions:** Similar to Section 269SS

### Consequences
- Penalty: 100% of amount
- Not disallowance in computing income

### Auditor Responsibility
Report in Form 3CD Clause 31: Amount of loan/deposit repaid in contravention.

## 8. TDS Compliance - Sections 194-199

### Key TDS Provisions Relevant to Tax Audit

**Section 194A - Interest (Non-Banking):**
- Threshold: Rs. 5,000 per annum (Rs. 40,000 for senior citizens)
- Rate: 10%

**Section 194C - Payments to Contractors:**
- Threshold: Rs. 30,000 (single payment) or Rs. 1 lakh (aggregate in year)
- Rate: 1% (individual/HUF), 2% (others)

**Section 194H - Commission/Brokerage:**
- Threshold: Rs. 15,000 per annum
- Rate: 5%

**Section 194I - Rent:**
- Plant & Machinery: Rs. 2,40,000 (2% rate)
- Land/Building/Furniture: Rs. 2,40,000 (10% rate)

**Section 194J - Professional/Technical Fees:**
- Threshold: Rs. 30,000 per annum
- Rate: 10% (2% for technical fees u/s 194J(b))

**Section 194Q - Purchase of Goods (>Rs. 50 Lakh turnover buyer):**
- Threshold: Rs. 50 lakh aggregate purchases from single seller
- Rate: 0.1%
- Effective from July 1, 2021

**Section 194R - Benefits/Perquisites (Business/Profession):**
- Threshold: Rs. 20,000 per annum
- Rate: 10%
- Effective from July 1, 2022

### Auditor Responsibilities (Form 3CD)
- Clause 33: Whether TDS/TCS deposited in time? If not, amount and reason
- Clause 34: Details of tax deducted/collected and paid
- Common audit procedures:
  - Match TDS returns (26Q/27Q) with books
  - Verify challan payment dates vs due dates
  - Check 26AS for TDS credit confirmation
  - Identify payments requiring TDS but not deducted

**Common Issues:**
- Late deposit of TDS (interest u/s 201(1A) applicable)
- Short deduction due to wrong rate/threshold interpretation
- Non-deduction on payments like rent, professional fees
- Misclassification (e.g., contractor payment treated as purchase)

## 9. Section 115BAA/115BAB - Concessional Tax Rates for Companies

### Section 115BAA (Domestic Companies)
- Rate: 22% (plus surcharge & cess = effective ~25.17%)
- Conditions:
  - Opt-in is irrevocable
  - No MAT credit can be claimed
  - Several deductions/exemptions not available (80C to 80IA, depreciation u/s 32(1)(iia), etc.)

### Section 115BAB (New Manufacturing Companies)
- Rate: 15% (plus surcharge & cess = effective ~17.16%)
- Conditions:
  - Incorporated on or after October 1, 2019
  - Commenced manufacturing by March 31, 2024 (extended by Finance Act 2023)
  - Does not use plant/machinery previously used
  - Similar restrictions as 115BAA

### Auditor Responsibility
- Verify if company opted for concessional regime
- Check compliance with conditions if opted
- Report tax rate applied in computation

## 10. Minimum Alternate Tax (MAT) - Section 115JB

### Applicability
Companies (other than those opting for Section 115BAA/115BAB).

### Mechanism
- Book profit u/s 115JB adjusted for specified items
- Tax @15% (plus surcharge & cess = effective ~17.47%)
- Payable if regular tax < MAT
- MAT credit available for 15 years (carry forward)

### Auditor Focus
- Reconcile book profit per financials to MAT profit
- Verify adjustments u/s 115JB Explanation 1 (depreciation difference, provisions, unabsorbed losses, etc.)
- Assess MAT credit computation and carry forward
- Report in Form 3CD Clause 37

## 11. Alternate Minimum Tax (AMT) - Section 115JC

### Applicability
Non-corporate taxpayers claiming certain deductions u/s 80H to 80RRB (except 80P), 10AA, 35AD.

### Mechanism
- Adjusted total income computed by adding back deductions
- Tax @18.5% (plus surcharge & cess)
- AMT credit available for 15 years

### Auditor Responsibility
- Verify AMT applicability and computation
- Check AMT credit calculation

## 12. Form 3CD - Key Clauses Quick Reference

### Critical Reporting Areas

**Clause 10:** Valuation of opening and closing stock
**Clause 13:** Capital expenditure ≥ Rs. 10,000 (under one head) claimed as revenue
**Clause 17:** Capital asset conversion to stock-in-trade (and vice versa)
**Clause 18:** Related party transactions (nature, amount, reasonableness)
**Clause 20:** Unexplained investment, money, expenditure u/s 69 to 69D
**Clause 21:** Details of deemed income u/s 32 to 44DA
**Clause 23:** Amounts payable u/s 43B not paid before due date
**Clause 26:** Payments disallowable u/s 40A(3) [cash ≥ Rs. 10,000]
**Clause 30A:** Compliance with transfer pricing provisions (u/s 92 to 92F)
**Clause 31:** Violations of Sections 269SS, 269T, 269ST
**Clause 32:** Amount borrowed/loans advanced on interest-free/interest-bearing basis
**Clause 33:** TDS/TCS deposit timeliness
**Clause 34:** TDS/TCS details and credits claimed
**Clause 42:** Details of donations u/s 80G

### Common Qualifications/Remarks
- "Subject to confirmation" for payables/receivables not confirmed
- "Subject to physical verification" for inventory not observed
- TDS/43B compliance issues
- Related party transaction disclosures
- Cash transaction violations

## 13. Common Tax Audit Issues by Area

### Revenue
- TDS on receipt (if applicable - e.g., contractor receipts u/s 194C)
- Cash receipts violating Section 269ST
- Revenue recognition timing (accrual vs receipt)

### Expenses
- Disallowances u/s 40A(3) - cash payments
- Disallowances u/s 43B - unpaid statutory dues
- TDS compliance on payments
- Related party payments u/s 40A(2)

### Capital vs Revenue
- Clause 13 reporting for capital expenditure claimed as revenue
- Depreciation rates applied vs Block of Assets classification

### Statutory Compliance
- PF/ESI payment vs ECR filing timing
- GST input credit claimed vs paid
- Professional tax payment

### Cash Transactions
- Section 269ST violations (receipts)
- Section 269SS/T violations (loans)
- Section 40A(3) violations (payments)
