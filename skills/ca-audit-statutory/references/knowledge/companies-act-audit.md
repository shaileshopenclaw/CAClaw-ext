# Companies Act 2013: Auditor Provisions Reference

## Overview

Sections 139 to 148 of the Companies Act 2013 govern the appointment, qualifications, powers, duties, rotation, remuneration, removal, and penalties related to statutory auditors of companies.

**Key Principles:**
- Auditors are appointed by shareholders (not management)
- Independence is paramount - multiple disqualifications ensure auditor independence
- Mandatory rotation for certain companies to prevent familiarity threat
- Specific duties and powers conferred by statute
- Civil and criminal penalties for non-compliance

---

## Section 139: Appointment of Auditors

### Subsection (1) - First Auditor

**Provision:** Every company shall, at the first annual general meeting, appoint an individual or firm as an auditor who shall hold office from the conclusion of that meeting till the conclusion of its sixth annual general meeting.

**Key Points:**
- **First auditor's term:** Up to 6th AGM (not 5 years - counts AGMs, not years)
- Appointed by members in first AGM
- If first AGM not held within 90 days of incorporation, Board may appoint first auditor within 30 days of due date for first AGM

**Exception:** Government company - Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) appoints auditor within 180 days of registration; if not appointed, Board appoints.

---

### Subsection (2) - Subsequent Auditors

**Provision:** At every AGM, a company shall appoint an individual or a firm as auditor to hold office from conclusion of that meeting until conclusion of next AGM. Auditor may be reappointed at AGM if:
- Not disqualified
- Not withdrawn eligibility
- Received certificate from company that appointment is per Act requirements
- Filed Form ADT-1 with Registrar within 15 days of appointment

**Key Points:**
- **Subsequent auditor's term:** 1 year (from conclusion of one AGM to conclusion of next AGM)
- Reappointment requires member approval each year (except where rotation applies)
- Auditor must file consent in Form ADT-1 within 15 days

---

### Subsection (4) - Auditor Rotation for Listed and Specified Companies

**Provision:** Listed companies and other prescribed companies (public companies with paid-up capital ≥ Rs. 10 crores or public companies with paid-up capital < Rs. 10 crores but turnover ≥ Rs. 50 crores) shall rotate auditors as follows:

**Individual Auditor:**
- Maximum tenure: 1 term of 5 consecutive years
- Cooling-off period: Cannot be reappointed for 5 years after completion of term

**Audit Firm:**
- Maximum tenure: 2 terms of 5 consecutive years (i.e., 10 years total)
- Cooling-off period: Cannot be reappointed for 5 years after completion of maximum tenure

**Calculation of Tenure:**
- Tenure calculated from date of appointment
- Includes continuous period - if auditor appointed mid-year, partial year counts as 1 year
- If auditor appointed in 2020 for 5 years, rotation applies from 2020 to 2024 (5th year); new auditor from 2025

**Transitional Provisions:**
- Existing auditors at time of notification (2014) could continue till completion of current term; rotation to be followed thereafter
- If existing auditor completed ≥ 1 term of 5 years or 2 terms of 5 years (for firms), cooling-off period started from FY 2014-15

**Exception:** Private companies not required to rotate auditors.

---

### Subsection (5) - Company to Inform Auditor of Appointment

**Provision:** Company shall inform auditor concerned of their appointment and file notice of appointment with Registrar within 15 days.

**Form:** Form ADT-1 (Notice of appointment of auditor)

---

### Subsection (6) - Casual Vacancy in Office of Auditor

**Provision:** Any casual vacancy in office of auditor shall:
- **If auditor appointed by members:** Be filled by Board within 30 days; auditor so appointed holds office until conclusion of next AGM
- **If auditor appointed by CAG (government company):** Be filled by CAG within 30 days; if not filled, Board fills vacancy

**Casual Vacancy Arises Due To:**
- Resignation
- Death
- Disqualification
- Insolvency

**Practical Implication:** Company must act promptly to fill vacancy to ensure continuous audit coverage.

---

### Subsection (8) - Auditor's Right to Attend General Meetings

**Provision:** Auditor shall have right to:
- Attend any general meeting
- Receive all notices and communications relating to general meeting
- Be heard at general meeting on any part of business concerning them as auditor

**Practical Implication:** Auditors should attend AGMs where financial statements are adopted or where matters affecting audit are discussed.

---

### Subsection (9) - Retiring Auditor's Right to Make Representations

**Provision:** If auditor proposes to resign or does not seek reappointment, they may make written representations to company. Company shall:
- Send copy of representations to every member (if received in time)
- If not received in time, auditor may require representations be read at meeting

**Practical Implication:** Provides auditor a forum to raise concerns before disengagement.

---

### Subsection (10) - Penalty for Non-Compliance

**Provision:** If company contravenes provisions of Section 139:
- Company: Fine between Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs
- Officer in default: Fine between Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1 lakh

---

## Section 140: Removal and Resignation of Auditor

### Subsection (1) - Removal of Auditor

**Provision:** Auditor appointed under Section 139 may be removed from office before expiry of term only by special resolution of company, after obtaining prior approval of Central Government.

**Procedure:**
1. Company applies to Central Government in Form ADT-2 for approval to remove auditor
2. Government may grant approval after giving auditor reasonable opportunity to be heard
3. Company passes special resolution in general meeting
4. Company informs auditor and Registrar in Form ADT-3 within 30 days

**Grounds for Removal (Evaluated by Government):**
- Professional misconduct
- Fraud
- Non-performance of duties
- Conflict of interest

**Practical Implication:** High threshold for removal protects auditor independence.

---

### Subsection (2) - Where Application Made to Central Government

**Provision:** Where application for removal made, auditor shall not resign until application is disposed of by Central Government.

---

### Subsection (4) - Resignation of Auditor

**Provision:** Auditor may resign from office by giving written notice to company and filing statement in Form ADT-3 with company and Registrar.

**Statement (Form ADT-3) Must Contain:**
- Reasons for resignation
- Other relevant facts and information

**Timeline:** Within 30 days of resignation

**Company's Obligation:**
- Convene Board meeting within 30 days to consider resignation
- Send copy of resignation to Registrar within 30 days along with explanation if Board disagrees with reasons

**Practical Implication:** Auditor cannot simply resign without providing reasons - ensures transparency.

---

### Subsection (5) - Penalty for False or Incorrect Statement in Form ADT-3

**Provision:** If auditor files false or incorrect statement in Form ADT-3:
- Auditor: Fine between Rs. 50,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs
- If continuing failure: Additional fine of Rs. 500 per day

---

## Section 141: Eligibility and Qualifications of Auditors

### Subsection (1) - Who Can Be Appointed

**Provision:** Only a person who is a Chartered Accountant within meaning of Chartered Accountants Act 1949 may be appointed as auditor of a company.

**Individual or Firm:** Can be individual CA or firm of CAs.

---

### Subsection (2) - Where Firm Appointed

**Provision:** Where firm (including LLP) appointed as auditor:
- Only partners who are CAs shall be authorized to act and sign on behalf of firm
- Majority of partners must be practicing in India

---

### Subsection (3) - Disqualifications

**Provision:** Following persons are disqualified from being appointed as auditor:

**3(a) - Body Corporate**
- No company or body corporate can be appointed as auditor

**3(b) - Officer or Employee**
- Officer or employee of company cannot be auditor

**3(c) - Partner/Employee of Officer or Employee**
- Partner or employee of an officer or employee of company cannot be auditor
- Practical example: If director's spouse is CA practicing with firm, that firm disqualified

**3(d) - Indebted Person**
- Person indebted to company for > Rs. 5 lakhs, or guarantor for any third person indebted > Rs. 1 lakh, is disqualified

**3(e) - Related Party**
- Person who is related party of director or key managerial personnel (KMP) cannot be auditor
- "Related party" includes relative, partner, employer/employee of director/KMP

**3(f) - Convicted of Offence**
- Person convicted by court for offence involving fraud and 10 years not elapsed is disqualified

**3(g) - Holding Securities**
- Person holding securities of company (except shares held by virtue of subscription to rights issue or bonus issue while qualifying shares held prior to appointment) is disqualified

**3(h) - Specified Services Provider**
- Person who renders certain specified services to company is disqualified, including:
  - Accounting and book-keeping services
  - Internal audit
  - Design and implementation of financial information system
  - Actuarial services
  - Investment advisory services
  - Investment banking services
  - Rendering outsourced financial services
  - Management services
  - **Exception:** These services can be rendered to holding/subsidiary if approved by Board/audit committee

**3(i) - Business Relationship or Specified Relationship**
- Person having business relationship with company, or specified relationship with person in (b) to (g) above, is disqualified

**3(j) - Relative as Director or KMP**
- Person whose relative is director or in employment of company as director or KMP is disqualified

**Practical Implications:**
- Auditor must ensure no disqualifications exist at appointment and throughout tenure
- If disqualification arises during tenure, auditor must resign immediately
- Companies must obtain declarations from auditors regarding disqualifications

---

### Subsection (4) - Partner Disqualification Affecting Firm

**Provision:** If partner of auditor's firm is disqualified, firm ceases to be qualified for appointment unless disqualified partner retires from firm.

---

### Subsection (5) - Penalty for Non-Compliance

**Provision:** If auditor contravenes provisions:
- Auditor: Fine between Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs

---

## Section 142: Remuneration of Auditors

### Subsection (1) - Remuneration Fixed by Members

**Provision:** Remuneration of auditor appointed by members in general meeting shall be fixed by members or in manner directed by them.

**Common Practice:**
- Members may authorize Board to fix remuneration
- Resolution passed: "Remuneration of auditors shall be determined by the Board of Directors"

---

### Subsection (2) - Remuneration Includes

**Provision:** Remuneration includes:
- Fees for audit services
- Out-of-pocket expenses incurred in connection with audit
- Expenses for services for taxation, management consultancy, and other services

**Practical Implication:** Separate disclosure required for audit fees vs. non-audit fees.

---

### Subsection (3) - Penalty

**Provision:** If company contravenes:
- Company: Fine between Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs
- Officer in default: Fine between Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 1 lakh

---

## Section 143: Powers and Duties of Auditors

### Subsection (1) - Rights of Auditor

**Provision:** Every auditor of company has right to:

**(a)** Access at all times to books, accounts, and vouchers of company (whether at head office or elsewhere)

**(b)** Require and obtain from officers such information and explanations as necessary for audit

**(c)** Obtain information and explanations from officers relating to any branch office

**(d)** Inspect any branch office

**(e)** Require subsidiaries to provide information if parent company is holding company

**(f)** Right to attend general meetings and be heard

**Practical Implication:** Auditors have extensive rights to information - management cannot deny access.

---

### Subsection (2) - Auditor to Inquire Into Specific Matters

**Provision:** Auditor's report shall state whether in auditor's opinion:

**(a)** Company has kept proper books of account as required by law

**(b)** Proper returns adequate for audit received from branches not visited

**(c)** Company's balance sheet and profit & loss account are in agreement with books and returns

**(d)** Financial statements comply with accounting standards

**(e)** Any disqualifications in director appointment (based on outstanding notice u/s 164(2))

**(f)** Adequate internal financial controls exist and operating effectiveness

**(g)** Any other matters prescribed by rules

**Practical Implication:** These are minimum mandatory inquiries - auditor must report on each.

---

### Subsection (3) - Auditor's Report and Standards on Auditing

**Provision:** Auditor's report shall comply with auditing standards (Standards on Auditing issued by ICAI).

---

### Subsection (12) - Reporting Fraud to Central Government

**Provision:** If auditor of company, in course of audit, has reason to believe that offence involving fraud is being or has been committed against company by officers or employees, auditor shall:

**Step 1:** Report matter to Board/audit committee immediately (if fraud amount < Rs. 1 crore for listed companies or < Rs. 1 crore for other companies)

**Step 2:** If Board/audit committee does not respond within 45 days or auditor not satisfied with response, report to Central Government in Form ADT-4 within 15 days

**Fraud Thresholds (as per rules):**
- If fraud amount ≥ Rs. 1 crore: Report directly to Central Government (no need to wait for Board response)

**Penalty for Non-Compliance:**
- Fine between Rs. 1 lakh to Rs. 25 lakhs

**Practical Implication:** Auditor has statutory duty to report fraud - failure attracts penalty.

---

### Subsection (13) - Penalty on Auditor for Contravention

**Provision:** If auditor contravenes Section 143:
- Fine between Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs
- If continuing failure: Additional fine up to Rs. 500 per day

---

### Subsection (14) - CAG's Right to Conduct Supplementary Audit

**Provision:** Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) may, in case of company owned or controlled by Central/State Government, conduct supplementary audit and comment on or supplement auditor's report.

**Practical Implication:** Government companies subject to additional CAG scrutiny.

---

## Section 144: Auditor Not to Render Certain Services

### Prohibited Services (Overlaps with Section 141(3)(g))

**Provision:** Auditor shall provide to company only such services as approved by Board or audit committee. Following services cannot be rendered:

1. Accounting and book-keeping services
2. Internal audit
3. Design and implementation of financial information system
4. Actuarial services
5. Investment advisory services
6. Investment banking services
7. Rendering of outsourced financial services
8. Management services
9. Any other services as prescribed

**Exception:** Permitted to holding/subsidiary companies if approved by Board/audit committee.

**Practical Implication:** Ensures independence by preventing audit firm from becoming management's arm.

---

## Section 145: Auditor to Sign Audit Report

### Subsection (1) - Signing Requirements

**Provision:**
- **Individual auditor:** Sign in their own name
- **Firm:** Audit report shall be signed by partner(s) practicing in India in their personal name and in name of firm, with membership number and firm's registration number

**Practical Implication:** Ensures accountability - specific CA takes responsibility for audit opinion.

---

### Subsection (2) - Penalty for Non-Compliance

**Provision:** If auditor contravenes:
- Auditor: Fine between Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs

---

## Section 146: Auditor to Attend General Meeting

### Subsection (1) - Notice to Auditor

**Provision:** All notices and communications to members shall also be sent to auditor.

---

### Subsection (2) - Auditor's Right to Be Heard

**Provision:** Auditor entitled to attend general meetings and be heard on matters concerning them as auditor.

**Practical Implication:** Auditors should attend AGM to answer member queries on financial statements.

---

## Section 147: Punishment for Contravention

### Provision

**Provision:** If auditor, audit firm, or partner contravenes provisions with knowledge and intention to deceive company, creditors, or members:

**Punishment:**
- Imprisonment up to 1 year AND/OR
- Fine: Minimum Rs. 1 lakh, extending to Rs. 25 lakhs

**Application:** Criminal liability for intentional fraud or gross negligence.

---

## Section 148: Cost Audit

### Subsection (1) - Maintenance of Cost Records

**Provision:** Central Government may direct certain companies in specified industries to maintain cost records. Company shall maintain such records and get them audited by Cost Accountant.

**Applicability:** Companies with turnover ≥ Rs. 35 crores and aggregate turnover from products subject to cost audit ≥ Rs. 25 crores.

**Industries Covered:** Includes regulated products like electricity, pharmaceuticals, telecom, fertilizers, sugar, petroleum, etc.

---

### Subsection (2) - Cost Auditor Appointment

**Provision:** Every company to which cost audit applies shall appoint Cost Auditor on recommendation of audit committee (or Board if no audit committee).

**Remuneration:** Fixed by Board on recommendation of audit committee.

---

### Subsection (3) - Qualifications and Disqualifications

**Provision:** Cost Auditor shall be Cost Accountant in practice. Disqualifications similar to those for statutory auditors apply.

---

### Subsection (4) - Cost Auditor to Report to Central Government

**Provision:** If Cost Auditor detects fraud during cost audit, reporting obligations similar to Section 143(12) apply.

---

### Subsection (5) - Penalty

**Provision:** If company or officer or Cost Auditor contravenes:
- Fine between Rs. 25,000 to Rs. 5 lakhs
- If continuing: Additional fine up to Rs. 500 per day

## Quick Reference: Key Thresholds and Limits

| Provision | Threshold/Limit | Applicability |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Rotation - Listed companies** | 1 term (5 years) for individuals; 2 terms (10 years) for firms | Listed companies |
| **Rotation - Other specified companies** | Same as above | Public companies with paid-up capital ≥ Rs. 10 crores OR turnover ≥ Rs. 50 crores |
| **Exemption from rotation** | No rotation required | Private companies |
| **Indebtedness disqualification** | > Rs. 5 lakhs (auditor indebted) or > Rs. 1 lakh (auditor as guarantor) | All companies |
| **Fraud reporting - Direct to Govt** | ≥ Rs. 1 crore | All companies |
| **Fraud reporting - via Board first** | < Rs. 1 crore | All companies |
| **Cost audit applicability** | Turnover ≥ Rs. 35 crores + product turnover ≥ Rs. 25 crores | Specified industries |
| **Private company exemption (CARO)** | Paid-up capital ≤ Rs. 50 lakhs + revenue ≤ Rs. 5 crores + loans ≤ Rs. 50 lakhs | Private companies |

## Common Compliance Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Appointing First Auditor

**Facts:** ABC Private Limited incorporated in January 2025. First AGM held in August 2025.

**Compliance:**
1. Members appoint auditor in first AGM (August 2025)
2. Auditor's term: From conclusion of August 2025 AGM to conclusion of 6th AGM (likely held in 2031)
3. Company files Form ADT-1 within 15 days of appointment
4. Auditor files consent in Form ADT-1

---

### Scenario 2: Auditor Rotation - Listed Company

**Facts:** XYZ Limited (listed) appointed ABC & Co. (audit firm) in 2018.

**Compliance:**
1. ABC & Co. can continue for 2 terms of 5 years each = 10 years (2018-2027)
2. After 2027, ABC & Co. must be replaced
3. Cooling-off period: ABC & Co. cannot be reappointed until 2032 (5 years after 2027)
4. Different firm must be appointed from 2028 onwards

---

### Scenario 3: Auditor Resignation Mid-Year

**Facts:** Auditor of PQR Limited resigns in October 2025 citing disagreements on revenue recognition.

**Compliance:**
1. Auditor submits written resignation to company
2. Auditor files Form ADT-3 with company and Registrar within 30 days, stating reasons
3. Board convenes meeting within 30 days to consider resignation
4. If Board disagrees with reasons, files explanation with Registrar within 30 days
5. Board appoints new auditor within 30 days to fill casual vacancy
6. New auditor holds office until conclusion of next AGM
7. Members appoint auditor for subsequent term at next AGM

---

### Scenario 4: Fraud Detection and Reporting

**Facts:** During audit of LMN Limited, auditor discovers embezzlement of Rs. 75 lakhs by CFO.

**Compliance:**
1. Fraud amount < Rs. 1 crore → Report to Board/audit committee immediately
2. Board has 45 days to respond
3. If Board does not respond or auditor not satisfied with response, report to Central Government in Form ADT-4 within 15 days
4. If fraud amount was ≥ Rs. 1 crore → Report directly to Central Government in Form ADT-4 (no need to wait for Board)
