# CA workflow taxonomy

> Historical note: This page was migrated from the former CAClaw fork. Read "CAClaw" here as the OpenClaw CA add-on unless the page is explicitly describing that old fork history.


Phase 4 organizes the starter workflow pack into six recurring Chartered Accountant work areas. These domains are guidance and preparation surfaces, not promises of autonomous filing or statutory correctness.

## The six starter workflow domains

### GST

Use for GST preparation, review, reconciliation, filing readiness, information requests, and recurring compliance follow-up.

Typical work includes:

- outward and inward supply review
- return-preparation checklists
- reconciliation and variance follow-up
- client data request lists

### TDS

Use for TDS preparation, deduction review, due-date readiness, exception tracking, and supporting checklists.

Typical work includes:

- deduction review and follow-up
- return-preparation readiness
- challan/supporting information checks
- recurring compliance reminders

### Income tax

Use for income-tax preparation, client information gathering, review structure, and explanatory work.

Typical work includes:

- personal or business tax preparation checklists
- missing-information request lists
- review prompts before filing
- planning notes and follow-up questions

### Audit and review

Use for audit planning, workpaper preparation, evidence requests, review structure, and recurring assurance support.

Typical work includes:

- engagement kickoff checklists
- document request lists
- review prompts and workpaper scaffolds
- exception tracking and follow-up

### Financial reporting

Use for month-end, year-end, statement preparation, schedules, note drafting, and reporting review.

Typical work includes:

- close support checklists
- schedule and note-preparation scaffolds
- review prompts for statements and disclosures
- management-information packaging

### ROC and commercial-law support

Use for company-law compliance preparation, ROC filing readiness, document collection, and recurring statutory follow-up.

Typical work includes:

- compliance calendars and readiness checks
- filing support checklists
- information and document request lists
- review prompts before submission

## Domain rules

- Keep workflow content practical and reusable.
- Focus on preparation, review, and organization.
- Keep all workflows human-in-the-loop.
- Do not imply autonomous filing, legal guarantees, or government-portal automation.

## Relationship to later phases

- Phase 4 creates starter workflow content and packaging.
- Phase 5 can organize these domains around client-wise and compliance-wise work and future integration rails.
